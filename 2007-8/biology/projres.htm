Biology

 

Today, using the topic list you generated earlier this week (or using the list of topics posted to the web site), you will pick three topics that are interesting to you.  In order to be practical topics for our long-term project, it must be possible for you to limit the topics in the following ways:

1.      There must be some biological aspect of the topic that you could measure using scientific tools and equipment that we have access to

2.      You must be able to conduct your study during class times (we will generally have 45 minutes per week to work on the project)

3.      You must be able to make your measurements during December, January, and February

4.      Anything you need to use during your study must be safe for high school students and approved by your instructor

5.      Your topic must be especially relevant to people who live in Corvallis or the Willamette Valley (yourself included)

6.      Your general topic MUST be approved by your instructor before you begin your research

 

Today, you should accomplish the following tasks:

1.   Find a partner

2.   Make a list of three topics you would be interested in studying (you can use the list posted on my web site as a reference)

3.   For each of these topics, do the following in order to generate some information that you could use to help you narrow down your topic ideas:

a.   Find four credible internet sources that help you address one of the points above (ask if you need help evaluating your sources!)

b.   For each source, copy the URL into a Microsoft Word document

c.   In one or two paragraphs, summarize some relevant information from each source (this should be in your own words – do NOT cut and paste!)

d.   Copy and paste the contents of this document into an e-mail and send it to dan.bregar@corvallis.k12.or.us – the subject line should have your class period and the names of both partners in your group (if you are working on two different computers, it is OK to send two documents with the same subject)
