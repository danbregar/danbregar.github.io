Habitat Description Activity

Biology

 

Part I:

Working with a partner, identify four different types of habitats in the area specified below.  (You do not need to know the “real” scientific name of the habitat.)  Draw a map that shows the location of the habitats.  Label your map with a  visual description of the habitat, including the types of biotic (living) and abiotic (non-living) factors you can see.

 

Part II:

For TWO of your habitats, draw a food web to illustrate some of the interactions you might see in that habitat.  Your food web should show how food energy moves around in your habitat as organisms eat things or are eaten.
