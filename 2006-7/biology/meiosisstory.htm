Meiosis Storyline

For this assignment you are required to write a storyline about the process of meiosis.  Keep it short and simple. You may create fictional characters to help describe what happens, draw a comic, use song lyrics, or an analogy but must include:

·         The title of each phase

·         At least one caption per phase of meiosis I, the major actions in each phase are listed below and can be found in your text on Figure 9-17.page 194-195.

Meiosis Summary:

Meiosis I

Prophase 1: Replicated chromosome pairs “find each other” in the cell to from tetrads.

Metaphase 1: Tetrads line up in the center of the cell and prepare to divide. The initial pairing of the tetrads is so close that the chromosome arms can temporarily tangle and swap genetic material.

Anaphase 1:  Half the chromosomes move to one end, ½ to the other end of the cell.

Telophase 1: Cell wall or membrane forms in the middle of the dividing cell creating 2 haploid cells.

Meiosis II (happens simultaneously in each of the 2 cells produced in Meiosis I)

Prophase II: Starts with a single set of chromosomes (each made up of a pair sister chromatids linked by a centromere).

Metaphase II: Chromosomes line up in center of the cell.

Anaphase II: Sister chromatids separate and begin to migrate to opposite ends of the cell.

Telophase II: Cell wall or membrane forms in the middle of the dividing cell creating 4 gamete cells (sex cells)

Mitosis Storyline Example:

 

 

 

1. Interphase

Starts with single cell: When we first got married it was great, it was like we were one person!

Replication of DNA: But then she moved in and I noticed we suddenly had twice as much stuff, and the funny thing was she had exact copies of the same stuff I had.

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Prophase

Chromosomes Condense: First we tried to condense our stuff, pack it together to organize it. But it didn’t help things, we began to fight day and night, this cell wasn’t big enough for the both of us!

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Metaphase

Chromosomes Line up on Metaphase Plate: I want a divorce, I screamed! We saw a lawyer and began to divide up our belongings equally. She got half of everything I owned.

 

 

 

 

Anaphase

Daughter Chromosomes (replicates) separate and migrate away from each other:

Watching her go, dragging copies of all of my stuff behind her, I couldn’t help but remember the good times. Breaking up is hard to do.

 

 

 

 

 

 

Telophase:

Cell wall or membrane grows between the parent and daughter cell: I closed the door behind her as she left, there was a wall between us now and I knew we would never be as close as we once were.
