Seminar Research Day 1

Biology

 

Please keep in mind that seminars:

1.   Should be controversial, biological topics

2.   Should be presented in an objective, unbiased way

3.   Should include all relevant viewpoints

4.   Should be based on reliable, unbiased sources

5.   Should not be evaluative (i.e., you should not judge the merits of the different viewpoints)

 

Today, you should accomplish the following tasks:

1.   Find a partner

2.   Make a list of three topics you would be interested in studying (you can use the list posted on my web site as a reference)

3.   Check with Mr. Bregar to see which of your topics is available

4.   Begin researching your topic:

a.   Find ten credible internet sources

b.   For each source, copy the URL into a Microsoft Word document

c.   In one or two paragraphs, summarize some relevant information from each source (this should be in your own words – do NOT cut and paste!)

d.   e-mail this document as a Word attachment to dan.bregar@corvallis.k12.or.us – the subject line should have your class period and the names of both partners in your group (if you are working on two different computers, it is OK to send two documents with the same subject)
