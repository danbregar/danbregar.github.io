Meiosis Overview
  • Biology
2
What is Meiosis?
  • Meiosis creates four new cells from one original cell
  • The new cells are used for reproduction – they are gametes (sex cells)
  • In mammals, gametes are eggs and sperm
3
What Happens?
  • Meiosis only happens in organisms that reproduce sexually -  so it only happens in organisms that have genetic material from both mom and pop
  • Mom’s DNA is a complete set of chromosomes.  So is Pop’s.
  • So every sexually-reproducing organism has two versions of each chromosome!
4
What Happens?  (continued)
  • Before meiosis, DNA replication has occurred - this means that there are two copies of Mom’s chromosomes and two copies of Pop’s chromosomes
  • During meiosis, all those copies are separated out – instead of all the different versions and copies being in one cell, they are distributed into four new cells
5
Vocabulary Details
  • Cells that have two different version of each chromosome - one of Mom’s and one of Pop’s - are called “diploid”.  (Like our original cell.)
  • Cells that have only one version of each chromosome are called “haploid”.  (Like our four new cells.)
6
Important Points
  • Every chromosome can be identified as having come from either Mom or Pop (maternal or paternal DNA)
  • Every chromosome can be identified by the specific genes they contain – we use numbers to identify these chromosomes
  • For example, in humans, chromosome 1 has a gene that helps determine brain size – and chromosome 3 has a gene that helps determine blood type
  • Humans have 23 different chromosomes – since we have one complete set from Mom and one complete set from Pop, that makes 23 pairs of chromosomes
7
Diploid?  Sister Chromatids?
  • Sister chromatids are exact, identical copies of the same sequence of DNA
  • Diploidy occurs when two unique versions of the same chromosome are in a cell – they have the same genes, but contain different information (hair color, for example…)
8
Your Job
  • Use string, paper, paper clips, and markers to model the starting and ending points of meiosis
  • Start by showing a diploid cell with four chromosomes from Mom and four chromosomes from Pop – these chromosomes should already have been replicated, so they will appear as sister chromatids (make all attachments loosely!)
  • Each chromosome should be clearly identifiable by number (1, 2, 3, or 4) and parental source (Mom or Pop)
  • Show an instructor and get checked off before you continue!
  • Now, rearrange the genetic material to create four new haploid cells
  • Each cell should have one full set of chromosomes
  • Show an instructor again to make sure you’ve got it right!
