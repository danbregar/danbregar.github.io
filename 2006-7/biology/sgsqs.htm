My review questions:

 

A scientist was studying a population of vomiting squids (instead of spewing ink when threatened the spew…well…you know). The scientist took notes about their traits and DNA samples from each squid.  She found that 80% of the population had the allele for vomiting and 20% did not have the allele for vomiting and thus did not vomit.

 

Since vomiting squids are fascinating the scientist devoted her life to making observations about the population. After several years and a few squid generations she returned and took the same observations. This time 60% of the population vomited, and 40% did not.

 

1)      Is there genetic variation for a trait in this population

2)      Did evolution occur?

3)      Did natural selection occur?

 

1.   Evolution and its associated theories provide a scientific explanation for how so many diverse forms of life could exist:  as organisms reproduced, and gene frequencies in populations changed, some populations of organisms ultimately evolved into entirely new species.  If this happened millions of times over billions of years, it could explain the diversity of life.  Now imagine that you are the director of a research institute that studies evolution.  Design a long-term study that will last 10,000 years, to test the idea that evolution could lead to the development of a new species from an existing species. Please write your response in at least 1-2 paragraphs.  (nature of science, evolution, speciation)

Make sure to be clear about:

a)      What kind of organism/organisms would you study, why?

b)      What kind of predictions or hypotheses would you make, based on what prior knowledge or evidence?  

c)      What data would be collected and how?

d)      What data would support or reject the theory that evolution had happened?

Class definitions:

Evolution= any change in allele frequencies over time

 

Adaptation= any HERITABLE trait or behavior obtained through NATURAL SELECTION over generations that help individuals survive in their environment.

 

Natural selection= A mechanism of evolution that predicts that individuals in a population that are better adapted will survive, reproduce, and pass on their genes to the next generation. Natural selection acts on existing genetic variation in a population.

 

Acclimatization= Non-heritable changes in the behavior of an individual that help them get used to a new environment. These are changes that are under the control of the individual, they can choose to change.

 

Population- any group of the same species of organism that live in the same area and interbreed.
