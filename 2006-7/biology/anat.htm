ANATOMY AND PHYSIOLOGY

 

Doctors and scientists have been investigating how bodies work and function for 100s of years. Discoveries were made from dissections of human cadavers and the dissection and collection of organisms from all over the world.

 

Even back in the 1700s scientists kept discovering curious structures that were mysterious in their purpose. They found similarities in the function of some structures that came from very different organisms. Then they found structures that looked similar but functioned somewhat differently. There were even more similarities when scientists examined very young, developing organisms. What could explain these patterns?

 

 

 

 

Your task: Imagine your are a scientist in the early 1800s. You discover some structures that seem to serve no function in its present day organism:

1)    Appendix- The human appendix is a small pouch attached to the large intestine where it joins the small intestine

2)    Erector pili(goose bumps)- these are sets of smooth muscle under the skin that control the actions of hair on your body, causing them to stand up or lay down

3)    Wisdom teeth- The molars in human found in the very back of the mouth. They grow in about the time you enter high school.

 

Come up with a hypothesis describing:

1)    How these structures may have functioned in the past (how could they have been useful).

2)    Why these structures remain in modern day organisms.

(There are no wrong answers here, just be thoughtful- ask yourself where are they located, could they protect some part of the body, is there another part of the body that is near or associated with it that may do the same job?)

 

3) Then get online and find out what scientists say these structures might have been used for and compare them to your hypotheses.

 

·        Define vestigial structure in your notes and list an example.

·        Define homologous structures and list/draw an example.

·        Define analogous structures and list/draw and example.

Hint: Work in groups, divide up the questions then share your results.
