VARIATION

 

We talk a lot about variation, but what kinds of traits can vary? How much? Your goal today will be to check out the amount of variation that exists for a trait of your choice. Specifically, let’s find out how individuals of the same species vary.

 

VARIATION IN HUMAN FINGERS

You would expect the length of an adult human’s index finger to be different in men compared to women because men have larger body sizes.  But, there is also variation in index finger length between individual men as well as between individual women.  Believe it or not, several different genes operate in our bodies to control finger length from when we are a fetus until the time we stop growing.

Your job is to measure the length of the index fingers of several of your classmates then create a graph of the variation in the finger length of your classmates.

 

Steps:

1.    Predict: Will one length of the trait be more common? If so what do you predict it would be and why?

2.    Use the rulers to measure the length of your classmates’ index fingers, to the nearest centimeter, from the base of the finger to the tip of the finger.  To be consistent, measure the finger on your classmates’ dominant hand (the hand they write with).  

3.    Repeat your measurements for as many of  your classmates as possible, recording your data on scratch paper as you go.  Split the measuring task among your group members in order to work efficiently. Try not to disturb the other groups.

4.    With your partner, draw a bar graph that places finger length in centimeters along the bottom axis (X-axis) and counts of classmates in with each length along the side axis (Y-axis).  Scroll down to see an example graph.

5.    Compare your prediction with your results – was there a most common length?  If so, what was it and why do you think it was the most common?
