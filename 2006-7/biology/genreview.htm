Applications

  1. Be able to describe in several sentences how Mendel’s principals of segregation, and independent assortment, relate to the process of meiosis.
  2. Given a set of chromosomes in a parent cell, you should be able to identify the # of chromosomes, chromatids, sister chromatids, and homologous pairs.
  3. Given a set of chromosomes in a parent cell, you should be able to show me what the daughter cells of meiosis 1 and meiosis 2 look like with respect to the chromosomes.
  4. You should be able to complete simple single trait Punnett squares for traits displaying dominance or incomplete dominance.
  5. Be able to work a Punnett square to predict offspring genotypes and phenotypes given:
    1. The phenotypes of the parents (ex/ incomplete dominance- trait=coat color, homozygous recessive white coat x heterozygote grey coat)
    2. The genotypes of the parents (ex/ Parent 1= HH, Parent 2= hh, trait= hair texture, recessive allele= straight, dominant=curly)
    3. Or given any mixture of vocabulary (see #6)
  6. You should be able to use the vocabulary of genetics (genotype, phenotype, heterozygote, homozygote, recessive, dominant, allele, gene, trait) to develop complete answers to questions on the exam as well as interpret data given on the exam.
  7. You should be able to show your work, interpret questions and determine what is being asked, try hard, and ask for clarification when needed.

 

Big tip: I give partial credit. So the more you can use and master vocabulary, give examples, and show your work or thought processes the more chance you have to get credit for what you know. Just make sure that what you know relates to the question being asked.
