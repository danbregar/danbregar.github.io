Protist Graph

Biology

 

Today, your goal is to create a graph that you will use to compare your predicted populations of protists to the actual populations that you find.  This is an INDIVIDUAL activity – you will be working with the data that you and your partner collected yesterday, but each of you should have your own spreadsheet and graph by the end of the period.

 

1.   Start by opening up a Microsoft Excel spreadsheet.  Create the following columns:  “Date”, “# of Protists (Actual)”, “# of Protists (Predicted)”.

 

2.   In the “Date” column, enter the following dates:  11/2/06, 11/7/06, 11/9/06, 11/14/06, 11/16/06.

 

3.   Using your data from yesterday, calculate the average number of protists you saw on each slide – to calculate this, add up the total number of protists you and your partner saw and divide that total by the number of slides you looked at.  For example, if you and your partner saw a total of 325 protists and you looked at 18 slides, the average number of protists per slide that you saw would be 325 / 18 or 18.05.  This number should be the first number in your columns for both “# of Protists (Actual)” and “# of Protists (Predicted)”.

 

4.   Create a graph for the predicted number of protists.  Do this by clicking on the “Date” heading and dragging the mouse to highlight all the dates in the first column.  Then, press and hold the “Ctrl” key.  While holding this key down, click on the “# of Protists (Predicted)” heading and drag the mouse down to highlight all the cells (including the blank cells) in that column.  Then, click on the “Graph” button and follow the prompts to create a line graph.

 

5.   Finally, enter some hypothetical numbers in the “# of Protists (Predicted)” column.  Your goal is to make your graph match up with the graph that you drew last week as your prediction – so you will need to experiment with different numbers until you end up with a graph that is as close to your prediction as possible.
