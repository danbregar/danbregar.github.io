Here’s the catch: Lots of organisms produce more young than they need to replace themselves….

  • Salmon produce 2000- 10,000 eggs
  • Maple trees produce 12,000-91,000 fertilized seeds
  • Bluebirds have 4-5 chicks per clutch per year
  • Sheep have 1-2 lambs each year starting at 8mo old.
  • Female elephants that spend years raising young have (on average) 1 calf every 5 years after they turn ~13 years old.

Lets Talk Limits!

Observation 1: Resources (food, space, water, shelter) are limited.

Observation 2: Organisms produce excess offspring

 

Result 1: Competition (if you can’t beat em’ eat em’)!

Competition: is the interaction of two or more organisms (or species) that results in an increase in birth and survival rates of one at the expense of the other.

 

 

Result 2: Symbioses (if you can’t beat em’ join em’)!

 

 

Now lets face it, competition is rough (and potentially life threatening, so it’s advantageous for organisms to specialize in using resources that other species don’t)

 

Niche: full range of physical and biological conditions in which an organism lives and the way in which the organism uses those conditions.

 

Analogy of niche: An organism’s job- Where does it work? What does it do? Who does it work with? Who does it compete with? When does it clock in and out? Etc.

 

Observation 3: Competition is strongest between organisms that occupy similar to same niches- No two species can occupy the same exact niche in the same habitat at the same time.
