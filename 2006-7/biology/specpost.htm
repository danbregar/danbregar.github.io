Speciation Poster Project Guidelines

 

You will be working in groups of 2 to complete this project. You are free to select your own partner for this project. Hint: make your speciation stories scientifically plausible!

 

Your grade will be determined by:

25% - presentation (10pts)

25%- student evaluation (5pts)

50%- content (15)

Create a Poster Project

Use your research you gathered about the speciation to create your own poster project to inform the class about the speciation events that led to the adaptive radiation of the Weebles.  You will need to show what mechanisms were at work to create the adaptations that arose over time and how changes in the environment (competition, habitat, predation) may have driven those changes. You will also need to show how your species diverged (divided) into distinct groups and give reasonable scientific hypotheses as to how populations became isolated enough to form distinct gene pools. Finally, you will need to explain which reproductive isolating mechanisms may have kept species separated. If you have a species that has undergone extinction, you should explain how that happened. You will also need to cite at least two references at the bottom of your poster project.  You will have two class periods to work on your project.  

You will need to include:

_______A title (large enough font to read from 4 ft away)

 _______A diagram of your part of the phylogenetic tree

________Drawings of your organisms with descriptions of their adaptations

________Names for each species of organism on your poster

________A timeline of major speciation events (hint, evolution can take a long time)

________A paragraph per step describing:

  • What changed in the environment (habitat, predation, or resource competition) that provided selection pressure?
  • What adaptations developed as a result of selection?
  • What isolating mechanisms influenced speciation and how?
  • If one of your species went extinct, explain how that happened.

 

Your poster should be neat, attractive, organized, and easy to follow.
