4.   Be able to use information about succession within a specific ecosystem – you should be able to make predictions about what MIGHT happen and explain why certain things DO happen for a variety of situations – for example

a.   In a forest ecosystem, small grasses and flowers are often followed by shrubs, which are followed by trees in a typical successional pattern.  So trees represent the climax community.  If this is the case, when would we ever see small grasses and flowers or shrubs?  In other words, what might cause trees to disappear and the successional pattern to start over?

b.   In looking at the slide show on Bald Hill, there was a clear pattern to the types of vegetation we saw.  If you were looking at two different areas in the Willamette Valley that were similar to Bald Hill, and one of them was covered with shrubs while the other one was covered with oak trees and a few Douglas Firs, which site do you suppose was left untouched by humans or other disturbances for a longer period of time?
