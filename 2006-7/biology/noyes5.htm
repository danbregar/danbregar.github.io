Noyes Property Spittle Bug Investigation

Field Biology5th period

 

* What is the relationship between the number of spittle bug nests and the type of plant species they are found on?

 

 

Materials Needed:

  • Dichotomous key to identify surrounding plant species
  • Equipment for measuring plots and quadrants (1m x 1m)
  • Clippers
  • Data Sheets
  • Flagging/site markers
  • Magnifying glass/hand lens

 

General Research Questions:

  • Describe the niche of the common spittle bug. In other words:

Where do they live?

What do they eat?

What eats them?

How long do they live?

What kind of insect are they?

How do they make their nests?

Why do they make their nests? What purpose does the nest serve? Who makes it?

Where do they tend to make their nests?

 

  •  What plant species are (likely to be) at the Noyes property?  (Look at plant lists for McDonald Forest and at the Willamette Gardens website.)
  • What was the Noyes property like before today?
  • Are there multiple species of spittle bugs on the Noyes property? How could we identify them?
  • What do we do about unidentifiable species of plants?
  • How will we know when we’ve collected “enough” data? What would an adequate sample size be?
  • What are some additional variables (environmental, ecological, human) that might affect the results we see? How could we control those variables?
  • How might the data from this study be useful to members of the community, caretakers of the Noyes property, members of the scientific community, or future field biology classes?
  • What do you think the relationship will be between the number of spittle bug nests and the species of plant they are found on?

 

Data Sheet:

  • # of spittle bug nests/plant
  • Date / Time
  • Team
  • Plot #
  • Quadrant ID
  • Species Checklist
