            Glossary of terms

  • Dominant: term given to an allele (version of a gene) that is always expressed when present.
  • Recessive: an allele that is usually only expressed when a dominant gene is NOT present
  • Gene: instructions found on the chromosome for a particular characteristic.
  • Genotype: the gene pair for a particular trait
  • Phenotype: the physical expression of a gene
  • Homozygous dominant: HH genotype (having 2 of the same allele, both dominant)
  • Homozygous recessive: hh genotype (having 2 of the same allele, both recessive)
  • Heterozygous dominant: Hh genotype (having 2 of the same allele, dominant allele is expressed)
  • Incomplete dominance: a gene pair that results in a trait somewhere between the traits of the 2 parents. Ex/ Hh genotype, both alleles are expressed equally.
