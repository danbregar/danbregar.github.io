Phospholipid Bi-layer Activity Questions

Biology

 

(Adapted from “Self-Assembly of Real Cell Membranes” by Gary Gaulin)

 

Mix together about 50 mL of water and about 10 mL of cooking oil.  Shake these two ingredients together, then respond to the following:

 

1.   Immediately after shaking the two substances, how did the oil and water appear to be mixed together?

2.   How long did it take for the oil and water to completely separate?

3.   After the two substances separated, describe their appearance.

 

Now, add one drop of egg yolk by piercing the egg yolk with an eye dropper, taking an extremely small sample of the yolk, and placing one drop in your mixture. 

 

4.   What happens to the drop of egg yolk when you add it to your mixture?

 

Now, shake the ingredients together again, then respond to the following:

 

5.   Immediately after shaking the mixture, how did the substances appear to be mixed together?

6.   How long did it take for the mixture to stabilize?

7.   After it stabilized, describe the appearance of the mixture.

8.   Using your understanding of phospholipid bi-layers, explain how the egg yolk caused the water and oil to mix together in such a way that stable bubbles (or “vesicles”) are formed.
