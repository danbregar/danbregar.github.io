Evolution Poster

Biology

 

For the final project of the year, you will work in groups of two or three to create a poster that illustrates the main concepts of evolution we have discussed during the school year.  You may use a poster board or you may use Microsoft PowerPoint to make your poster (if you use PowerPoint, you do NOT need to print your poster out, but you do need to follow the special criteria below).  You will have half the period on Thursday, June 7 and all of the period on Tuesday, June 12 to complete your posters.  You will present your poster to the rest of the class and submit an evaluation of other students’ posters on Thursday, June 14.  Your poster will be graded on how well you address the scientific issues in the overview (below), how well you meet the specific requirements (below), and the overall appearance of your poster.

 

Overview:

In your poster, you should pick a specific example of evolution.  You can choose from one of the examples below (please sign in with your instructor to avoid duplication) or you may choose your own example.  On your poster, you should have an illustration and a short paragraph corresponding to each of the following:

1.   Give a brief overview of your example, including relevant descriptions and definitions.

2.   How is your example a demonstration of evolution?  Specifically describe how your example fits the definition of evolution as “a change in allele frequencies of a population over time”.

3.   How does your example show natural selection?

4.   Does your example demonstrate speciation?  If so, explain the details of how that speciation occurred.  If not, explain how speciation MIGHT occur in the future and predict what types of evolutionary changes might be seen.  Make sure to address the concept of reproductive isolation.

 

Specific Requirements:

1.      Use either a large piece of white poster board or Microsoft PowerPoint.  For PowerPoint posters, please do the following:

·         Go to Page Set-up and select a paper size of 48” wide by 36” high.  This is known as “E-sized” paper.

·         Use white backgrounds behind black text – this is generally the easiest to read.

2.      There should be a title in a large font at the top center of your poster.

3.      Locate on the bottom, left hand corner of your poster a “Revised:  DATE” line and the name of the file.  Use a small font size for this. 

4.      While you want to be complete in your description, do not include excessive amounts of text.  The text should be sized so that information is easily read from eight to ten feet away.

5.      Be sure to spell check and proofread.  THIS IS CRITICAL.  Points will be deducted for simple mistakes.

6.      Use the posters that are posted in the hall of the science department as examples.

 

Keep in mind that when most people read your poster, very few will start with the title and then work their way through the poster in the order listed below.  The eyes of your reader will generally be drawn to large text, graphs, pictures, and diagrams.  Ensure that these things stand alone as much as possible.
