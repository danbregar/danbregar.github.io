Review Questions: Genetics Unit Test

Questions and Definitions

  1. What are traits, genes, and alleles?
  2. What are Punnett squares used for?
  3. What 2 processes do Punnett squares model?  (Hint – where do the parental alleles come from?  What are we illustrating when we combine these alleles in a Punnett square?)
  4. What are alleles?
    a) genes for different traits, such as hair color or eye color.
    b) alternative forms of a gene for a single trait, such as blue eyes or brown eyes.
    c) the location of genes on a chromosome
    d) recessive forms of a kind of characteristic carried by genes.
    e) dominant forms of a kind of characteristic carried by genes.
  5. If a pea plant shows a recessive phenotype?
    a) it can be either TT or tt.
    b) it can be either Tt or tt.
    c) it can only be TT.
    d) it can only be tt.
    e) it can be TT, Tt, or tt.

 

 

Applications

  1. Given a set of chromosomes in a parent cell, you should be able to show me what the daughter cells of meiosis 1 and meiosis 2 look like, and how these relate to the alleles shown in a Punnett Square.
  2. You should be able to complete simple single trait Punnett squares for traits displaying dominance or incomplete dominance.
  3. Be able to work a Punnett square to predict offspring genotypes and phenotypes given:
    1. The appearance (phenotypes) of the parents
    2. The genotypes of the parents (ex/ Parent 1= HH, Parent 2= hh, trait= hair texture, recessive allele= straight, dominant=curly)
    3. Or given any mixture of vocabulary (see #5)
  4. You should be able to use the vocabulary of genetics (genotype, heterozygote, homozygote, recessive, dominant, allele, gene, trait) to develop complete answers to questions on the exam as well as interpret data given on the exam.
  5. You should be able to show your work, interpret questions and determine what is being asked, try hard, and ask for clarification when needed.
