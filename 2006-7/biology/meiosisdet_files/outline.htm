Overview
  • Meiosis is a type of cell division in sexually reproducing organisms that results in cells with half the chromosome number of the original cell.
  • Happens in 2 phases: Meiosis 1, and Meiosis 2.
  • Phase 1 increase the amount of genetic variation & reduces chromosome #
  • Phase 2 separates sister chromatids
  • New cells created by meiotic division are gametes (egg and sperm cells in animals).
  • Mutations that occur in cells that perform meiosis are heritable (can be passed on from parent to offspring).
3
Meiosis 1: Prophase 1.
  • You start with replicated DNA
    • Each member of a homologous chromosome pair has copied itself (sister chromatids joined by a centromere)
    • Ex/ Chromosome pair 1 in a diploid organism is made up of 1 chromosome from mom, and 1 from dad. These are called homologous chromosomes. They each replicate creating 2 from mom, and 2 from dad.


  • Homologous chromosome pairs “find each other” in the cell to from tetrads.

Meiosis 1: Metaphase 1.
  • Tetrads line up in the center of the cell and prepare to divide.
  • The initial pairing of the tetrads are so close that the chromosome arms can temporarily tangle and swap genetic material.
    • This process is called crossing over
    • The net result is new combinations of genetic material!

Meiosis 1: Anaphase 1.
  • Tetrads (of homologous chromosomes) separate.
  • Half the chromosomes move to one end, ½ to the other end of the cell.

Meiosis 1: Telophase 1.
  • A cell wall or membrane forms between the cell’s 2 ends.
  • Result is 2 haploid cells
    • Each cell has 2 copies of only one chromosome from the original chromosome pair.

Meiosis 2 (Phase II of Meiosis)
  • During this phase, the 2 cells created in Meiosis 1 both divide their sister chromatids to create 2 new cells each (4 total)
  • Each new cell will contain the same amount of chromosomes as was present at the end of meiosis I.[( ½ the genetic material of the original sex cell (haploid)]

What Happens?
  • Before meiosis, DNA replication has occurred - this means that there are two copies of Mom’s chromosomes and two copies of Pop’s chromosomes (diploid + replicated)
  • During meiosis, all those copies are separated out – and copies are distributed into four new cells that will contain ½ the genetic material of the original sex cell and potentially new random combinations of genetic material.
