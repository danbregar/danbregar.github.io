1. Appearance

a.       Add photo of yourself

b.      Brief description (paragraph or bullets)         

c.       Any distinguishing characteristics/style?

 

2.  Habitat

    1. What kinds of places do you like to hang out at?
    2. What makes it “your type of place?”

                       

3.  Distribution or Range (must include map-google/yahoo)

    1. Migratory status/habits- Do you travel on breaks? Where do you go?
    2. Where are you commonly found?
    3. How far to you commonly travel (say in a typical week)?

 

4.  Social System

    1. How do you get along with others?
    2. Do you like working in groups, or alone?
    3. Patterns of dominance?
    4. What kind of behaviors are acceptable/unacceptable to you and what are the consequences?

 

5.  Diet

                                  a.      Carnivore/Omnivore/Herbivore

                                 b.      What are you commonly seen eating (your favorites)

                                  c.      Seasonal favorites?

 

6.  Cool Facts

a.       Interactions with other species (i.e. Mutualisms or Parasitisms? –pets, siblings) Explain.

b.      What else defines you? What else should we know about you? Sports? Hobbies?

7.   Conservation Status (optional)*

a.       Are you rare among other organisms in your habitat? Why?

Species Description Instructions

 

Ecologists and naturalists create species descriptions to help describe what a species looks like, where it can be found, and it’s behaviors. Today you will be constructing a mock “species description” of yourself!

 

The purpose of today’s activity is:

·        To introduce you to species descriptions

·        To help me learn more about you

·        To give us something fun and creative to do after spring break!

 

 

Instructions:

·       This assignment is worth points. It is due at the end of the period!

·       Make sure that your species description is about YOU, (not humans in general)

·       Your description must be FACTUALLY BASED, humorous extensions or elaborations are acceptable only if they are provided in addition to the facts.

·       As always, use appropriate language and content (no mating habits)

·       You may create your species description in digital form or hardcopy

·       Consult the template on Bregar’s Field Biology webpage for helpful hints and website examples. Include each of the headings listed in the template for full credit.

·       Length: at least 1-2 pages with pictures + text
