UNDERSTANDINGS

 

1.                  Although small and usually microscopic, the activities of microorganisms have a large influence on the health of our ecosystems.

2.                  In nature, organisms do not live by themselves, they have complex relationships by which they either cooperate or compete for resources to survive.  Lichens form the ultimate cooperative relationship, and their adaptations reflect complex energy and foodweb relationships in ecosystems.

3.                  The actions of humans are interwoven with our environment.  The actions of humans may negatively affect the environment, the health of which affects humans.  Lichens can be used as a bioassay of environmental health.

ESSENTIAL QUESTIONS

 

1.                  What features of algae and fungi make the lichen symbiosis possible? What factors influence natural selection for the lichen symbiosis? 

2.                  Lichens are a symbiosis of an alga and a fungus, but their classification is based on the fungal partner.  Why aren’t they classified by the algal partner instead?

3.                  Why don’t all fungi and algae become lichens?  Are there other, similar symbioses found in nature?

4.                  Fungi are generally known to be parasites or saprophytes.  As parasites they invade living plants and kill them.  As saprophytes, they break down dead plant materials.  What role do they have in the lichen symbiosis?

5.                  Where do lichens get their energy and where does this energy go in the ecosystem?

6.                  What special features of lichens cause them to be sensitive to air pollution.  How do lichens affect humans?
