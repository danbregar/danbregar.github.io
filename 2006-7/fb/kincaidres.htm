Kincaid’s Lupine Research

Field Biology

 

Today, you will do some background research related to the Kincaid’s Lupine project.  The work that you do today is going to relate to the work that you actually do throughout the year at either Fitton Green or Bald Hill Park; this is the first step of carrying out your actual project.  Your goal is to gather some information for background research that will be used as part of the introduction to your project proposal. 

 

Your background research today will focus on the following key ideas.  Your job will be to identify three different scientific web sites that address in detail each of the following concepts:

a.   Native plants of the Willamette Valley

b.   Non-native plants of the Willamette Valley

c.   Oak savannah landscapes in the Willamette Valley

d.   The relationship between Fender’s Blue Butterfly and Kincaid’s Lupine

e.   The use of fire as a management tool in oak savannah landscapes

 

For this assignment, you do not need to record any specific information – instead, simply write down the URL (the “http” address in your web browser) of three different web sites for each of these topics.  I recommend using Google to do your web searches.  You should identify only web sites that are associated with colleges, universities, government agencies, or research institutes.  In other words, web sites created by gardeners, private individuals, K-12 schools, or your average Joe-Bob are not acceptable sources.  (Here’s a hint – there are a number of full-text PDF scientific papers located on Dr. Mark V. Wilson’s web site that might relate to the project.)  You should end up with a total list of fifteen different web sites to choose from.
