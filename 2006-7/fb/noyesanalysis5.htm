To analyze the data you collected at the Noyes property, you will use a statistical test called a “t-test”.  I do not expect you to understand the mathematics behind this test!  However, I do expect that you will be able to compile the data we collected, arrange it in the proper format, and interpret the results of the t-test.

 

To begin with, copy the data we collected from the spreadsheet posted on my web site to a new, blank spreadsheet.  This data is arranged so that at every gridpoint, species that we observed to have spit bug nests are marked with the total number of nests per species of plant per site. Due to data recording error we do not know if the plants with 0 nests had 0 nests because they were not present in the site, or if they were present but no spit bugs chose to nest on them.  To correctly interpret this data, you will need to follow these steps:

 

1.   Regroup the data so that for each species of plant you remove all the zeros.

2.   Find the average number of nests per species of plant – place these in a summary data table. Construct a bar graph of these data.

3.   Arrange these averages in a data table that looks like this:

4.   To perform a t-test analysis, select a blank cell on your worksheet.  Click on the “Fx” (function) button below the toolbar.  From the “Statistics” category, choose “TTEST”.

5.   For the “array1” select the first column of data you wish to compare from a particular species (ex/vetch).  For the “array 2” select the data from the comparison species (ex/ grasses). For “tails” type in 2. For “type” type in 3. Click ok. The value for the t-test will appear in the box.

6.   To interpret your t test, use the following guidelines:

a.   If the result of your t-test test is 0.05 or less, then there was a statistically significant difference between the # of nests on the 2 compared species of plants.

b.   If the results of your t-test test is more than 0.05, then it is possible that any differences in your averages are merely due to chance and do not indicate that there is a real difference in the # of nests on the 2 species of plants.
