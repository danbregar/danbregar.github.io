- The “models quiz”:

1.   A model is a scientific description (mathematical or otherwise) that explains what we see and allows us to make predictions.

2.   We use models because a) they’re relatively simple and easy to use and b) we can’t always know the way things “really are”.

3.   If we discover a model is incorrect, we can change it (like we changed the tube models).  A model is only useful if it explains and predicts accurately – so if it doesn’t do that, we change or discard it.

 

- Predator:

1.   How do the numbers change?  (Demo what happens when we keep overall numbers even and what happens when we “pick on” certain colors.)

2.   In this game, “them that has, gets” – the colors that are most abundant grow faster than those that are least abundant.  So that can be our model for how the dots increase in number (since we don’t know the specific formulas used to calculate growth).

3.   We’re more likely to eat dots that contrast with the background color – so there’s nothing special about how any particular color of dot grows, but since we’re “picking on” colors that contrast, they’re less likely to be the most abundant.

 

- Relationship between models, tubes, antibiotic resistance, and predator:

1.   We developed models for all three activities – we don’t know how any of these things really work, but we were able to come up with potential descriptions.  Our descriptions might be COMPLETELY wrong – but as long as they explain and predict, we’re good.

2.   Antibiotic resistance and predator both represent some type of death and reproduction – bacteria are killed selectively by the antibiotic agent, so ones with resistance tend to survive and reproduce.  Dots are eaten selectively by the player – contrasting dots are more likely to be eaten, so camouflaged dots are more likely to survive and grow.

 

- All this has to do with “genetics”

1.   Our ideas about genes, inheritance, etc., are models – based on our observations.  They are pretty detailed models, and seem pretty accurate – but of course, like any other model, we can’t be sure they’re actually “the truth”.

2.   The basic model goes like this:  genes encode certain properties of organisms.  Since genes are heritable, offspring of a parent organism can potentially inherit the properties of that organism (the way that the offspring of bacteria can inherit resistance).
