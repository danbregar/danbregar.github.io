Proposal – Investigating the Relationship

between Plant Growth and Sunlight

 

Introduction

 

Question:

The purpose of this project is to determine the relationship between the growth rate of blue ryegrass (Elymus glaucus) and the average amount of daily sunlight received by the plant.  Our goal is to determine if there is any correlation between the weekly change in height of wild blue ryegrass plants and the amount of sunlight they get over the course of the day.  We will measure the growth of the blue ryegrass plants by measuring their height; we will determine the amount of sunlight they receive by using a light sensor that is attached to a laptop computer.

Hypothesis:

We believe that areas with greater sunlight will show more plant growth.  Sunlight acts directly as the energy source for photosynthesis (Wilson 1998).  In addition, areas in direct sunlight tend to be warmer than shaded areas, which increases metabolic activity in plants (Wilson 1998).  Finally, blue ryegrass is a plant that is adapted to upland prairies, which are generally limited in shade plants (Anderson 2004).  Therefore, we believe that the blue ryegrass will show the greatest growth rate in areas with more sunlight.

 

Methods:

To measure the growth of blue ryegrass, we will follow these steps:

1.   We will identify ten specific plants in each of our sites (we will have 4 sites total in each of our locations).

2.   Once a week, we will measure the height of these plants by stretching them to their full lengths and measuring the length of the stems using a ruler.

3.   Each week, we will calculate the growth rate of the plants using the following formula:  (New Height – Previous Height) / Previous Height.

(Cliuy 1993)

 

To measure the amount of sunlight, we will follow these steps:

1.   We will use a Vernier light sensor probe attached to a laptop computer.

2.   We will use LoggerPro 3.2 software to measure the amount of sunlight in Ergs.

(Wilson 1998)
