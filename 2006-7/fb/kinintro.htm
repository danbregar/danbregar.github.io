Part I:  Question generating

In class, we will come up with a list of potential “What is the Relationship Between” questions to use as the foundation for our research.  Our questions will fit the format that we developed earlier in the year – “What is the relationship between _____ of _____ and ____ of _____?”  Our question will center around two different factors, one of which will be Kincaid’s Lupine.  We will specify measuring words (phrases that indicate exactly what types of data we will collect) for each of the two factors.

 

Part II:  Introductory paragraphs

Using the web sites that you found last week, answer each of the following questions in one complete paragraph.  You will need to cite your work using in-text citations within the paragraph and a list of references following each paragraph.  In-text citation guidelines are located below, and the guide for creating reference lists from web pages is located at http://www2.corvallis.k12.or.us/cvhs/staff/westlaj/Library/APA_Style_Electronic_Citations.pdf. 

1.   Why are native plants an important part of Willamette Valley ecosystems (such as Chip Ross Park and Bald Hill)?

2.   How can non-native plants cause problems for Willamette Valley ecosystems?

3.   Why are oak savannah landscapes such a rare environment in the Willamette Valley?

4.   Explain why Fender’s Blue Butterfly and Kincaid’s Lupine are so closely linked ecologically.

5.   What are some of the advantages and disadvantages to using fire as a tool to create and maintain oak savannah landscapes?

 

In-Text Citation Format

If you know the author of the web site, your citaton should look like this:

(Bregar 2003)

If you don’t know the author, use the title in quotation marks (just use a little bit with an ellipsis if it’s long):

(“Arsenic and environmental…” 2001)

If you don’t know the year, leave it off:

(“Health effects of…”)

Here is an example of how an in-text citation would be used in a paper:

Arsenic is sometimes found in soils that developed through volcanic activity (“Arsenic and environmental” 2001).

In-text citations are required in your research, but they do not replace a full list of references – each paragraph should be followed by a list of references in proper format as shown on the CVHS library web site.
