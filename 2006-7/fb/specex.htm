The Common Bregar

 

Common Name: Dan Bregar

Species Name: Homo erectus

Other Names: Evil Bregar, Slimster

Appearance:

·         5’7”

·         Male

·         Caucasian

·         Eye Color –blue

·         Hair Color – unknown, experts maintain this species may be hairless

·         Commonly found in beanies during winter months, baseball hats in warmer seasons.

·         Often seen in shorts regardless of weather.

 

Photo of the Common Bregar attached to mutualist species, Canis domesticus rileyus.

 

Habitat:

This species prefers mountainous riparian areas and woodland trails. The Bregar prefers theses places for their solitude and beauty. The Common Bregar can be found in these areas songwriting and plotting his revenge on the Field Biology class.

 

Distribution and Range:                               Map of the Home Range of the Common Bregar

The Bregar can be found most often in the greater Corvallis area in Western Oregon. He spends most of his time in North Corvallis in a classroom teaching Field Biology which accounts for some of his sometimes-unpredictable behavior. His range extends south to the Bombs Away Café where he can be seen some evenings singing and performing on stage. The purpose for such displays are yet unknown, however, mating displays have recently been ruled out.  This species is a summer migrant. The Common Bregar has been known to travel the Pacific Northwest and roam into Central California and Sierra Nevada for weeks at a time.

 

Social System:

The Common Bregar is not often seen in large social groups. However, the Bregar can work well with others and is often seen teaching with the help of OSU students.  The Bregar exhibits strong patterns of dominance in the classroom. Interestingly, he is subdominant at home to both his girlfriend and his dog. The Bregar becomes most dangerous when provoked by other peoples’ laziness. Students in the Common Bregar’s class have been known to disappear as a result of his wrath.

 

 

Diet:

The Bregar is a species that needs to eat frequently to survive. The Bregar is an omnivore and can be found foraging for food at the Co-op or the CV cafeteria. He is commonly found feeding on cheese and crackers. When available this species also feeds on raw fish (Sushi). Scientists maintain this species is the first example of a cheese and sushi specialist.

 

Cool Facts:

The Bregar is a common to several species of parasites. The most common of those is the OSU student teacher. They can be found feeding on the ideas and lesson plans (and coffee supply) of the host Bregar. The Common Bregar also has a mutualism with a canine species, Canis domesticus rileyus, or the Common Riley. Riley provides inspiration for songs, protection, and companionship to the Bregar. The Bregar provides food and shelter to the Riley.  Field Biologist S. Barker has recently published a paper in this month’s The Naturalist noting the Common Bregar was seen exhibiting a new behavior. The Bregar has begun playing and studying the trombone and flute, could this behavior lead to a speciation event? Only time will tell.
