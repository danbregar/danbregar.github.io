1.   What is the scientific definition of evolution?  Is this a fact (something that can be and is observable) or is it a theory?

5.   Based on your answer to question #1 and our discussions in class, evaluate the accuracy of the following statement.

“Evolution … refers to the theory that the first living organism developed from nonliving matter.  Then, as it reproduced, it is said to have changed into different kinds of living things, producing ultimately all forms of life that have ever existed on earth, including humans.”

 

6.   Does the following statement make a case that evolution did NOT occur in this particular circumstance?  Explain your reasoning.

 “…the darker variety of peppered moth, which is said to be a mutant, survived better because it was difficult for birds to see them … the darker variety rapidly became the dominant type.  But was the peppered moth evolving into some other type of insect?  No, it was still exactly the same peppered moth, merely having a different coloration.”
