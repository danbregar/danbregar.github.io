Macroinvertebrate DO Tolerance Activity

Field Biology

 

Last week, we collected macroinvertebrate samples from two sites on Jackson Creek.  One of the sites was in fast moving water; the other was in a stagnant area of the creek.

 

1.   Which of these sites do you expect to be higher in dissolved oxygen (DO), and why?

 

As you have read, many macroinvertebrate organisms require high amounts of dissolved oxygen to survive, because they absorb oxygen directly from the water.  Other macroinvertebrates are less sensitive to DO because they have special adaptations that allow them to get the oxygen they need despite low levels of DO in the water.  (Some move to the surface periodically to get oxygen from the air; others are particularly efficient at removing DO; others have relatively low DO requirements.)

 

The following macroinvertebrate groups are highly sensitive to DO – in other words, they require relatively high amounts of DO to survive:  stoneflies, caddisflies, and mayflies.

 

These macroinvertebrate groups have less sensitivity to DO – in other words, they can survive with lower amounts of DO:  true flies, snails, beetle larvae.
