VISIBLE THALLUS STRUCTURE TERMS:

 

Thallus: The body of the lichen, composed of fungus and algae.  The thallus comes in 3 forms:

·        Crustose:  A crust thallus shape

·        Foliose: Thallus flat, leaf-shaped (from foliar, like a leaf)

·        Fruticose: Thallus trailing or tree-like, hanging down like a fruit

 

Cilia: Hairlike growths on the margin of the thallus of foliose lichens.

 

Rhizines: Root-like strands on the bottom surface of foliose lichens

 

MICROSCOPIC THALLUS STRUCTURE TERMS

 

Mycobiont – Fungus in the lichen.  “Myco” is an ancient word meaning fungus, “biont” is a biological partner.

 

Photobiont – Algae in the lichen.  “Photo” refers to the photosynthesis by the algae, “biont” is a biological partner.

 

Cortex: The outermost upper and lower layers of the lichen

 

Medulla: The middle layer of a lichen

 

Algal layer: The layer of the lichen that contains algae. 

 

REPRODUCTION  TERMS

 

Isidia:  Asexual reproductive structure.  Finger-like projections made up of small clumps of algae and fungus which when broken off can grow into a new lichen.

 

Soredia: Asexual reproductive structure. Powdery clumps produced by the lichen.  Each clump is composed of fungus and algae that can grow into a new lichen

 

Apothecium:  Sexually reproducing structure produced only by the fungus.  It produces ascospores that are shot into the air.
