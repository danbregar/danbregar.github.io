Momentum Problems 5

Physical Science and Technology

 

1.   A bowling ball with a mass of 5 kg is moving at 3 m/s.  What is its momentum?  Use the five steps.

 

2.   The bowling ball in problem #1 strikes a motionless bowling ball sitting in the middle of the street.  The first bowling ball comes to a complete stop, and the second bowling ball goes flying off.  The second bowling ball has a mass of 5 kg. 

a.   What is the momentum of the second bowling ball before the collision? (You do not need to use the five steps)

b.   What is the total momentum of both bowling balls combined before the collision?  (You do not need to use the five steps)

c.   What is the total momentum of both bowling balls combined after the collision (hint – use the law of conservation of momentum; you do not need to use the five steps)?

d.   What is the momentum of the second bowling ball after the collision?  (You do not need to use the five steps)

e.   What is the velocity of the second bowling ball after the collision (use the five steps)?

 

3.   Two bowling balls, each with a mass of 5 kg, are moving at 3 m/s.  What is the total momentum of both bowling balls combined?  Use the five steps.

 

4.   Imagine that the bowling balls in problem #3 strike two motionless bowling balls sitting in the middle of the street.  The first two bowling balls come to a complete stop, and the second two bowling balls go flying off.  The second two bowling balls each have a mass of 5 kg.

a.   What is the momentum of the second two bowling balls before the collision? (You do not need to use the five steps)

b.   What is the total momentum of all four bowling balls combined before the collision? (You do not need to use the five steps)

c.   What is the momentum of the second two bowling balls after the collision? (hint – use the law of conservation of momentum; you do not need to use the five steps)

d.   What is the total mass of the second two bowling balls after the collision?  (You do not need to use the five steps)

e.   What is the velocity of the second two bowling balls after the collision (use the five steps)?

 

5.   Imagine that the bowling balls in problem #3 strike a motionless bowling ball sitting in the middle of the street.  The first two bowling balls come to a complete stop, and the new bowling ball goes flying off.  The new bowling ball has a mass of 5 kg.

a.       What is the momentum of the new bowling ball after the collision?  (hint – use the law of conservation of momentum; you do not need to use the five steps)

b.   What is the mass of the new bowling ball?  (you do not need to use the five steps)

c.   What is the velocity of the new bowling ball after the collision (use the five steps)?
