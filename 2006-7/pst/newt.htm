Multiple Choice (you do not need to show your work; simply write the letter of the correct answer)

1.     The net force needed to accelerate an object is calculated by:

          a.   dividing mass by acceleration                                   b.     dividing mass by velocity

          c.   multiplying mass by acceleration                              d.     multiplying mass by velocity

 

2.     How much force would it take to accelerate a car with a mass of  500 kg car at a rate of 15 m/s/s?

          a.   0.03 N                                                                             b.     500 N

          c.   7500 N                                                                            d.     33.333 N

 

3.     A force is:

          a.   A push                                                                           b.     A pull

          c.   Something that can cause acceleration                    d.     All of the above

 

4.     The units for force are:

          a.   Newtons                                                                        b.     m/s/s

          c.   kg                                                                                    d.     All of the above

 

5.     When I drive my car, my foot pushes on the gas pedal.  According to Newton's Third Law, what also must be happening?

          a.   The car is speeding up                                                b.     The gas pedal pushes on my foot

          c.   Gasoline flows into the engine                                  d.     My leg pushes on my foot

 

6.     If you know an object's mass and the force used to push it, you can find the acceleration of the object using the following formula:

          a.   acceleration = mass x force

          b.   acceleration = mass ¸ force

          c.   acceleration = force ¸ mass

          d.   acceleration = mass + force

 

7.     According to Newton's First Law, it takes an unbalanced force to:

          a.   speed an object up                                                      b.     slow an object down

          c.   change an object's direction                                      d.     All of the above

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

 

Problems (answer on a separate sheet of paper):

1.   Chuck Justus is standing on top of a train that is travelling at 7 km/sec.  The train hits a cow and comes to a complete stop.  There is no gravity or friction in Chuck’s world.  What happens to Chuck?

 

2.   Jake Musick is using a cattle prod to push on Mr. Bregar's nose.  What, according to Newton's Third Law, must also be happening?

 

3.   A carnivorous giraffe throws Mr. Bregar straight up in the air in a location where there is no gravity. What happens to Mr. Bregar and why?

 

4.   Caleb Voldeba is using his hands to hold Mr. Bregar in the air above his head.  Mr. Bregar is not moving.  Mr. Bregar is standing on Caleb’s hands.  What, according to Newton's Third Law, must also be happening?

 

5.   How much unbalanced force would it take to accelerate Cindy Pacheco at a rate of  7.6 m/s/s if Cindy has a mass of 58 kg?  Please show the Five Steps.

 

6.   How much mass does Brett Branam have if it takes 269.3 N of unbalanced force to accelerate him 8.883 m/s/s?  Please show the Five Steps.

 

7.   What would the acceleration of Mr. Bregar be if he has a mass of 65 kg and he is being dragged behind a truck with a net force of 329 N?  Please show the Five Steps.
