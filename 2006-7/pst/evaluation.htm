Self Evaluation:

I think I did a pretty good job on this project.  My partner and I divided up the work as equally as we could.  We worked hard in class when we were given time, and we came in a couple of times during lunch to finish up our project.  The quality of our machine I think was very good - once we made all the changes and fixes at the end, it worked almost every time we ran it.  We also tried to do a good job of making our parts good quality.

The biggest problem we ran into was that we were never able to get our machine to actually fold the paper into the shape of a paper airplane.  We could get the sticks to hit the paper in the right place, but when the sticks hit the paper, it didn't fold - instead, it either wrinkled the paper or just knocked it off the machine.  In order to get the paper to fold right, we would have needed to create some pretty elaborate stands that held the paper in just the right position.  We tried to build these, but ended up running out of time.  However, I think we did the best job we could in the time available.

We also tried our best to take accurate measurements of time and displacement.  However, the parts often moved so quickly that we felt that we couldn't react fast enough to take accurate measurements.  Also, it was really hard to see exactly how far the different objects moved - again, sometimes they moved so quickly it was too hard to see precisely where they stopped.  So we just did the best we could with the equipment we had and I think we did a pretty good job.

Finally, I felt that I learned a lot about forces and motion by doing this project.  Building the RGM helped me see that forces can combine, and that an overall force can accuse an object to accelerate.  I also felt that I learned some new tricks for designing and building projects like these.  I feel more comfortable using the shop tools now, and I think I got much better and doing quality work.
