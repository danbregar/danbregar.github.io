Introduction:

A Rube Goldberg Machine (RGM) is a device that is designed to accomplish a simple task in an extremely complicated manner.  An examples of a Rube Goldberg Machine might be a device that pulls a piece of Kleenex out of a box by using a series of levers, pulleys, and ramps.  There are many different images of Rube Goldberg Machines posted at the web site http://www.rube-goldberg.com/.  Here is one example from that website:

This machine uses wind power to sharpen a pencil!

Rube Goldberg was an actual person who drew cartoons of Rube Goldberg Machines in the early 1900's.  Now, people compete to actually build Rube Goldberg Machines in a contest that occurs every year.  This year's contest is to take an orange, squeeze it, and pour the juice in a cup.

Rube Goldberg Machines are designed using simple machines - like ramps, levers, and pulleys - that make work easier.
