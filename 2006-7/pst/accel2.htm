Acceleration Problems II

Physical Science and Technology

 

Instructions:  Please complete these problems on your own sheet of paper – write your name, today’s date, and the due date (today) in the top left corner of your paper.  Write the title of this assignment (“Acceleration Problems 2”) across the top of your page.  Use the Five Steps where indicated.

 

1.         A gigantic rat is running along the street with a velocity of 7.2 m/s.  If it accelerates at a rate of 4.2 m/s2 for 11.5 s, what will its final velocity be?  Use the Five Steps.

 

2.         A skinny turkey is plummeting from the top of Mary’s Peak.  When it finally lands, its velocity is 752.1 m/s.  If it fell for 31.5 seconds, and it accelerated at 9.8 m/s2, what was its initial velocity?  Use the Five Steps.

 

3.         A highly intelligent tick is crawling along the top of Mr. Bregar’s skull.  When it first starts crawling, it is traveling at 0.05 m/s.  It accelerates at 0.01 m/s2 until it is traveling at 0.12 m/s.  For how long did it accelerate?

 

4.         A big piece of chicken is thrown across the road.  Before it is thrown, it is motionless.  When it lands on the other side of the road, its velocity is 5.6 m/s.  If it was in the air for 7.5 seconds, what was its acceleration?  Use the Five Steps.

 

5.         A ferocious dog named Riley is chasing a pigeon down the street.  If Riley starts running at 4.8 m/s and over a time of 11.5 seconds accelerates at 1.14 m/s2, what will her final velocity be?  Use the Five Steps.
