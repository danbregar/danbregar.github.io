Momentum Problems 4

Physical Science and Technology

 

Please use your own paper and use the Five Steps except where indicated.

 

1.   A cart that has a mass of 17 kg is rolling down the street with a velocity of 1.2 m/s.  Mrs. Zimmers, who has a mass of 61 kg, is riding in the cart.  She is carrying her favorite bowling ball, which has a mass of 9 kg.

a.   What is the momentum of the cart?

b.   What is the momentum of Mrs. Zimmers?

c.   What is the momentum of the bowling ball?

d.   What is the momentum of all these objects combined?  (You do not need to use the Five Steps for this part)

 

2.   Mrs. Stroud is running down the street with a velocity of 4.2 m/s. She has a mass of 62 kg.

a.   What is Mrs. Stroud’s momentum?

b.   As Mrs. Stroud is running, she sees a bowling ball sitting motionless in the street.  The bowling ball has a mass of 7 kg.  What is the momentum of the bowling ball?

c.   What is the combined momentum of Mrs. Stroud and the bowling ball?  (You do not need to use the Five Steps for this part)

d.   Mrs. Stroud, as she is running, picks up the bowling ball.  What is the combined momentum of Mrs. Stroud and the bowling ball together?  (hint – remember the law of conservation of momentum; you do not need to use the Five Steps for this part!)

e.   What is the combined mass of Mrs. Stroud and the bowling ball?  (You do not need to use the Five Steps for this part)

f.    What are Mrs. Stroud and the bowling ball’s velocity after she picks up the bowling ball (you DO need the Five Steps for this!)?

 

3.   A bowling ball is rolling down the street.  It has a mass of 6.8 kg and a velocity of 1.4 m/s.

a.   What is its momentum?

b.   There are two other bowling balls sitting still in the middle of the street that each have a mass of 5 kg.  What is their total momentum?

c.   What is the total momentum of all three bowling balls combined?  (You do not need to use the Five Steps for this part).

d.   The first bowling ball slams into the second two bowling balls.  The first bowling ball comes to a complete stop, while the other two bowling balls go flying off.  What is the total momentum of all three bowling balls combined?  (Hint - remember the Law of Conservation of Momentum; you do not need the Five Steps for this part!)
