Velocity Problems 2

Physical Science and Technology

 

Instructions:  Please complete these problems on your own sheet of paper – write your name, today’s date, and the due date (today) in the top left corner of your paper.  Write the title of this assignment (“Velocity Problems”) across the top of your page.  Use the Five Steps where indicated.

 

1.   A giant porpoise is swimming in the ocean.  If it swims with a velocity of 76.1 m/s and swims for 8.9 s, how far does it go?  Use the Five Steps.

 

2.   An ignorant orangutan is jumping through the trees.  If it jumps for 17.88 seconds and travels 87.1 meters, what is its velocity?  Use the Five Steps.

 

3.   A very friendly rhinoceros is heffalumping through the desert.  If it heffalumps for 8,183.2 meters and its velocity is 46.7 m/s, for how long was it heffalumping?  Use the Five Steps.

 

4.   A giant pigapotamus is eating a tray of cheesecake.  If it eats 87 meters of cheesecake at a velocity of 8 m/s, for how long was it eating?  Use the Five Steps.

 

5.   The pigapotamus from question 4 is chasing the rhinoceros from question 3.  If the pigapotamus is running at 8.77 m/s and it runs for 18.7 seconds, how far did it go?  Use the Five Steps.

 

6.   The orangutan from question 2 is riding on top of the porpoise from question 1.  If they swim for 11,788 m and travel for 8,222.1 seconds, what is their velocity?  Use the Five Steps.

 

7.   The porpoise from question 1, the orangutan from question 2, the rhinoceros from question 3, and the pigapotamus from question 4 are all running through Glen Canyon in Utah trying to catch Mr. Bregar, who has stolen the pigapotamus’ cheesecake.  If they all run with a velocity of 7.881 m/s and travel a total of 81,331.2 m, for how long were they running?  Use the Five Steps.
