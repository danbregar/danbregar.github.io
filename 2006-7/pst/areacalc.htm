Model Structure and Area Calculations

Physical Science and Technology

 

Your tasks for the day are as follows:

1.   If you did not finish your orthographic drawings, do this first!

2.   If you did not finish your scale layout drawings, do this as well!

3.   Using your scale layout drawing, create a paper model of your structure:

a.   Cut out the shapes (hint – make as few cuts as possible so you can use as little tape as possible!)

b.   Tape them together in the proper arrangement

c.   Remember – air will need to circulate freely through your structure in order to keep it inflated – so there will need to be air passageways that connect all of your shapes!

4.   After you’ve finished your model, check with your instructor for approval based on:

a.   The quality of your work

b.   The accuracy of your drawing, cutting, and taping

c.   The presence of air passageways

5.   After your model is checked off, calculate the total area of plastic you will need to create your structure

a.   Make a rough sketch of your scale layout drawing, with actual measurements (not scale measurements – to find your actual measurements, multiply your scale measurements by 10)

b.   Computer the area of each shape using the following formulas:  rectangles = length x width; triangles = 0.5 x base x height; circles = 3.14 x radius x radius

c.   Find the total area of your structure by adding the areas of each shape together
