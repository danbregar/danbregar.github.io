Inflatable Structures Layout Drawings

Physical Science and Technology

 

The next step in creating your inflatable structure is to make a drawing showing how you will cut shapes from your plastic in order to make your structure.  To do this, you’ll need to do the following steps:

 

1.   Make a quick sketch of your structure that uses the following only the following shapes:  cylinders, cubes, three-sided pyramids, and four-sided pyramids.

 

2.   By using the surface development examples handout (also posted on the web site), translate each of your shapes into rough layout drawings.

 

3.   Add size information to your rough layout drawings – each of you will have a sheet of plastic about 100 cm by 300 cm; all of your layouts should fit into this area.

 

4.   Make a final layout drawing to scale:  using the large computer paper provided, make drawings using rulers and circle templates.  On your drawings, one centimeter should represent 10 centimeters in real life (so a line that will be 120 centimeters in real life should show up as 12 centimeters long).
