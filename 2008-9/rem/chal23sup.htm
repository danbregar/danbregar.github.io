Challenge #2 and #3 Supplement

Recording, Engineering, and Mixing

 

On Wednesday, February 25, I will be out all day.  This is a great opportunity for you to make some revisions to Challenge #2 and to continue working on Challenge #3.  Here are some things that I noticed that nearly everyone could think about:

 

1.      Make sure to read the instructions for each challenge and make sure you’ve addressed ALL of the requirements.  For example, in Challenge #2, very few students used delay and reverb as “send effects”.   The instructions for doing this are posted here.  Using this technique is not simple – but it’s easy once you get the hang of it.  Go through the instructions for each challenge one by one and check that you have completed them all correctly.

2.      Double-check that you have correctly adjusted the threshold and ratio settings for compression.  Most of you left the ratio at 1.0 : 1 when you used compression in Challenge #2.  You can go back and fix this – make sure that the ratio is up around 4 : 1 and that the threshold is low enough to make the meter read between -3 and -6 db at the loudest parts of each track.

3.      Check the volume levels of each track.  At no point during a song should a track go “into the red” – the little red light above the track should never be lit.  If it does light up, turn the track down a bit.  Also watch the master volume – it shouldn’t go into the red either!  When track volume levels get to high, they become distorted and add a very unpleasant sound to the mix.

4.      Use the solo button to listen to each track.  If you’ve added an effect, you should be able to hear it.  You can click the check box next to the effect name to turn it on and off so you can compare how the track sounds with and without the effect.  If you can’t hear a difference when you turn the effect on and off, you either haven’t adjusted the effect parameters correctly or you should choose a different effect.

5.      Treat the “L” and “R” tracks as one instrument.  These letters indicate that the instrument was recorded with two mics – one placed on the left side, and one on the right side.  So, for example, the “Organ L” and “Organ R” tracks are the same instrument.  That means you should add the same effects to both of these!  The easiest way to do this is to create a new track and call it “Organ”.  Click on the folder symbol to make this a folder track.  Move the “Organ L” and “Organ R” tracks just below the new “Organ” track – then click on the folder symbol of the last track in the folder.  That will turn the folder symbol of that track red and “close” the folder.  (See the image below for an illustration.)  When you add compression and other effects, add them to the folder track instead of the individual instrument tracks.  That way, the effects will be applied to both the “L” and “R” components of the instrument.
                             

6.      Use your ears to determine what sounds good and what doesn’t.  Solo individual tracks or groups of tracks to really isolate the instruments.  When adjusting EQ or other effect parameters, listen carefully to make sure that the changes you make are actually improving the sound of the instrument.  Feel free to play around with the adjustments – but ultimately, you should be LISTENING carefully to each track and to the overall sound in order to determine what to keep and what to leave off.

7.      When you’re done, send me your RPP file as an attachment.  Many of you don’t have a grade for Challenge #2 because I never got your e-mail.  Use the “Save As” command to rename your project file as “yourfullname.rpp”, with no spaces.  For example, if I sent my file, it would be called “danbregar.rpp”.  My e-mail address is dan.bregar@corvallis.k12.or.us.  You can send it from your e-mail account or a friend’s – as long as the file is named correctly, I’ll know who to give the points to.

 

Again, the most critical thing is to make sure to read the instructions carefully for each challenge.  When you think you’re done, go back through the instructions step-by-step to make sure you didn’t forget anything.  If there’s something you’re not sure how to do, you can ask me or get help from another student.
