1.   A turtle is running down the street with a velocity of 11.45 m/s.  If it accelerates at 1.3 m/s for 16.3 s, what will its velocity be?  (Use the Five Steps).

 

2.   Draw a displacement vs. time graph that shows the motion of an object that moves in the following way:  starting at 4 meters, it moves 3 m backwards in 2 s.  Then, it moves 2 m backwards in 4 s.  Then, it moves 5 m forward in 2 s.  Then, it moves 4 m backwards in 4 s.  You can assume that each time it moves, it does so at a constant velocity.

 

3.   A ferret is walking with a velocity of 7.2 m/s.  If it travels 78.2 m, for how long was it walking?  (Use the Five Steps).

 

4.   Draw a velocity vs. time graph that shows the motion of an object that moves in the following way:  for 2 s, it moves backwards with a velocity of 2.5 m/s.  For 1 s, it moves forward 3 m.  For 2 s, it is motionless.  It then moves 3 m backwards in 2 s at a constant velocity.  Then, it moves 1 m forward in 3 s at a constant velocity.  Finally, it moves backwards at 2 m/s for 4 s.

 

5.   A bear is digging into the ground, tunneling after Mr. Bregar.  After 145.8 seconds of digging, his velocity is 2.187 m/s.  If he was accelerating at 0.015 m/s/s while he was running, what was his original velocity?  (Use the Five Steps).

 

6.   Draw a displacement vs. time graph that shows the motion of an object that moves in the following way:  starting at zero meters, it moves at 4 m/s for 2 s.  Then, it moves at -1 m/s for 3 s.  Then, it moves 6 m forward in 2 s.  Then, it moves 2 m forward in 3 s.  You can assume that each time it moves, it does so at a constant velocity.
