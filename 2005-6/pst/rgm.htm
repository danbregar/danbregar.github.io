Rube Goldberg Machine Design Challenge for PST

    Specifications:

    1. You and a partner must design and build a RGM that will receive a marble from a previous group and pass it on to the next group. These

        marbles will be used to trigger the next machine in the sequence. We will attempt to link all 36 individual machines together to create one large

        Rube Goldberg Machine.

    2. Your RGM must fit on  a base that is 30.5 cm by 30.5 cm (12" x 12"). It may be no more than 38cm (15") tall.

    3. It must receive the marble at one corner and release it at the opposite corner of the base. The only allowable extension beyond the edge of the

        base is at those corners and only for transfer of marbles from one machine to the next.

    4. Each group must include at least two (2) simple machines or mechanisms. Each of you will be responsible for and graded on one of these

        machines or mechanism.

    5. Each group's RGM must take a minimum of 2 seconds to complete its task. This will allow time to reset your device so we can try to make the

        large RGM repeat itself several times in succession. No other human intervention will be allowed.

    6. Careful craftsmanship will be required because your RGM will be expected to be reliable and predictable enough to operate many times in

        in succession without requiring repairs.

    7. Materials provided will include:

            Base- 3/4" particle board

            Wooden sticks- 6cm X 19 cm  and up to 40 cm long

            Aluminum offset plates (thin sheet metal)- be careful not to cut yourself

            Adhesives and fasteners

            Mousetrap if needed

            Clothes pins

            Steel welding rod- 3mm diameter

 

    Procedures:

    1. Generate ideas by drawing thumbnail sketches. Show them to one of the instructors.

    2. Work with your partner to chose a final design and produce drawings.

        a. Top view- one of you should draw this.

        b. Side view- the other should draw this one.

    3. Develop a Bill of Materials- make a list of the parts and supplies you will need. Indicate what you will bring from home and what you will need

        from the school.

    4. Build your machine and test it for reliability and functionality.
