Force Diagrams

Physical Science and Technology

 

Use your own paper to draw a force diagram for Mr. Bregar in problems 1-5 below.  Remember the rules for drawing force diagrams:

1)   Draw all the forces that are specifically described in the problem.

2)   Draw a pulling force for gravity (unless the problem says there is no gravity).

3)   If the object is on a surface, and that surface is either slowing the object down or preventing the object from sliding along the surface, draw a pushing force for friction (opposite the direction of motion).

4)   If the object is on a surface, and that surface is preventing the object from falling straight down, draw a pushing force for the surface force that is perpendicular (90 degree angle) to the surface.

 

Also remember that

a)   Force diagrams only show ONE object at a time (so in these problems, you should only draw Mr. Bregar).

b)   Pushing forces are shown by arrows pointing towards the object and pulling forces are shown by arrows pointing away from the object.

c)   All arrows should be clearly labeled.

d)   Only show forces that are acting ON the object (not forces that the object is exerting).

 

1.   Mr. Bregar is being stepped upon by an elephant.

 

2.   Mr. Bregar is in outer space, where there is no gravity, and a Martian is pushing him.

 

3.   Mr. Bregar is being squished by a vise.

 

4.   Mr. Bregar is being pulled in one direction by Brandon Powers and in the opposite direction by Max Lee.  Max is pulling twice as hard as Brandon is, so Mr. Bregar is sliding along the floor towards Max.

 

5.   Mr. Bregar is floating in outer space, where there is no gravity, and he is pushing on a rocket.

 

6.   In problems 1, 3, and 4, gravity is pulling on Mr. Bregar.  Newton’s 3rd law says that if gravity is pulling on Mr. Bregar, then Mr. Bregar must be pulling on gravity.  Does this make sense?  Can you explain what the "equal and opposite reaction" force is to the force of gravity pulling on Mr. Bregar?  (Hint - it’s the same for each problem, and it’s not a force that you would draw on the force diagram because it’s NOT acting on Mr. Bregar.)
