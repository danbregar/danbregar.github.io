Physical Science & Technology

Web Page Portfolio Assignment

 

 

OBJECTIVE:     To create and post a web page on the Internet that will serve as an online portfolio of your work throughout the semester.  This will also eventually become a primary communication tool with respect to upcoming projects.  Think of your web page as the means by which you are going to show the world what you have learned in this class. 

 

DETAILS:          For this web page you are to do the following:

1.       Set up a web page account at Geocities (the place where your web pages and other materials are going to live on the Internet).  Go to http://geocities.yahoo.com/home/.  Click on the link “free” under the “Get Started” heading.  Follow the instructions for creating your own Geocities Account. 

2.       Once you have created your account, you can create a web page and post materials online.  Use Microsoft FrontPage to create your main page.  Save your main page as index.html.  This will be the page people first go to when accessing your materials.  All of your other materials will be linked here.

3.       On your index page, create a place for the following (this place could be other web pages if you wish):

a)       Completed assignments

b)       Related Links to other web sites.

c)       Personal information about your interests.  Include some pictures, but remember to cite your source!

d)       Include a link to the CV Science Department Web Page:  http://www2.corvallis.k12.or.us/cvhs/science/index.htm .

e)       Include a link to the class web page:  http://www2.corvallis.k12.or.us/cvhs/bregard/pst.htm .

f)         Create links to your Inflatable Structure Project report, spreadsheet, and web page.  These should be listed under your completed assignments section.

g)       Upload your index page, any additional web pages you have made, and materials from last week’s assignment to your geocities account.  You want to do this by going to the “Easy Upload” under the “File Manager” tool at Geocities.

h)       When you know your web page is working, come to one of the instructors.  A link to your page will be created on the class web page.  In this way, we can verify that your link works.  Also, now all students (and teachers) will easily be able to access each other’s web pages.

4.       You will be given time periodically to work on your web pages in class.  However, some work may be required outside of class as the semester proceeds.

5.       Your web pages will be periodically checked and graded throughout the semester.

 

ASSESSMENT: Your grade for this project will be a composite score based upon what your teacher, your peers, and your parents think of your web site.  Your website will be graded based upon the following criteria:

·         Creativity (use of illustrations, links to related web sites, etc…).

·         Adherence to guidelines (that will be given as the project proceeds).

·         Accuracy of content placed upon your web site.

·         Inclusion of all topics covered in class.

·         Requirements regarding exactly what should be placed on your web page will be provided as the class proceeds.
