Study Guide – Force Diagrams and Net Forces

Physical Science and Technology

 

Use your own paper to draw a force diagram for the object specified in problems 1-3 below.  Remember the rules for drawing force diagrams:

1)   Draw all the forces that are specifically described in the problem.

2)   Draw a pulling force for gravity (unless the problem says there is no gravity).

3)   If the object is on a surface, and that surface is either slowing the object down or preventing the object from sliding along the surface, draw a pushing force for friction (opposite the direction of motion).

4)   If the object is on a surface, and that surface is preventing the object from falling straight down, draw a pushing force for the surface force that is perpendicular (90 degree angle) to the surface.

Also remember that

a)   Force diagrams only show ONE object at a time

b)   Pushing forces are shown by arrows pointing towards the object and pulling forces are shown by arrows pointing away from the object.

c)   All arrows should be clearly labeled.

d)   Only show forces that are acting ON the object (not forces that the object is exerting).
