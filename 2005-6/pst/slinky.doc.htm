Slinky Power!

 

This activity is designed to allow you to measure different properties of a slinky and see how they change as the slinky is moved and stretched.

 

The materials in this activity are a laptop computer with a current meter, a slinky, a ruler, a power supply, and cables to connect everything together.

 

Activity Guide:

1.  The laptop is set up to measure electrical current.  Current tells you how much electricity is flowing through a wire.  Current is measured in “Amperes”, which are abbreviated “Amps” or “A”.  The higher the number is, the more current is going through the wire.

 

2.  To change the amount of current going through the slinky, turn the knob on the power supply up.

 

3.  You can also stretch the slinky to see change the amount of current going through it.

 

Procedure:

Since you can change the amount of current going through the slinky, you can do an experiment to try to find out how stretching the slinky might change the amount of current going through it.

 

1.  Start by stretching the slinky out to 0.5 meters (using the ruler) and setting the power supply to 0.5 Amperes (ask for help if you need it!).

Questions:

1.  When the slinky had the most current going through it, how far was it stretched and what was the power supply setting?

 

2.  When the slinky had the smallest amount of current going through it, how far was it stretched and what was the power supply setting?

 

3.  How did stretching out the slinky change the amount of current going through the slinky?
