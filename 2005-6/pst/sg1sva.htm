Study Guide #1 – Velocity and Acceleration Quiz

Physical Science and Technology

 

1.   Draw a displacement vs. time graph that shows the motion of an object that moves in the following way:  starting at one meter, it moves 2 meters backwards in 3 seconds.  Then, it moves 4 meters forward in 1 second.  Then, it moves 2 meters forward in 2 seconds.  Then, it moves 3 meters backwards in 6 seconds.  You can assume that each time it moves, it does so at a constant velocity.

 

2.   Draw a velocity vs. time graph that shows the motion of an object that moves in the following way:  for 3 seconds, it moves forward with a velocity of 1.5 m/s.  For 2 seconds, it is motionless.  It then moves 5 meters backwards in 2 seconds at a constant velocity.  Then, it moves 6 meters forward in 3 seconds at a constant velocity.  Finally, it travels backwards with a velocity of 2 m/s.

 

3.   A duck is running down the street with a velocity of 2.78 m/s.  If it runs for 7.67 seconds, how far did it run?  (Use the Five Steps).

 

4.   Another duck is chasing after the first duck with a velocity of 1.3 m/s.  If it accelerates at 4.4 m/s/s for 4.9 seconds, what will its velocity be?  (Use the Five Steps).

 

5.   Mr. Bregar is running away from the ducks in problems 3 and 4.  After 15 seconds of running, his velocity is 57.6 m/s.  If he was accelerating at 1.4 m/s/s while he was running, what was his original velocity?  (Use the Five Steps).
