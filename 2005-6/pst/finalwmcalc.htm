THE MOTION

 

1.  What is the linear speed of the tips of your wind machine blades?  To find this, you will need to convert the revolutions per minute measurement you obtained from the strobe light into the linear velocity of the blade tip at a moment in time.  To do this, you will need to convert an angular speed to a linear speed.

(RPM) x (2) x (3.14) x (r) / 60 = linear speed in meters/second.

3.  What is the linear speed (m/sec) of a point on the surface of your shaft?

 

4.  What is the acceleration of the mass as it is lifted from the ground to your final height above the ground.  Hint:  What is the initial velocity of the mass?  What is the final velocity of the mass?  How long did it take to lift the mass this distance?

 

 

FORCE AND ENERGY FOR THE LIFTED MASS

 

5.  Draw a force diagram of the mass as it is lifted from the ground to the final height.  Indicate larger forces with longer arrows.  In addition, place the size of each force on your diagram.

 

6.  Using your acceleration you calculated in #4, determine the unbalanced force your wind machine exerts on the mass.

 

7.  Determine the force gravity is exerting on the mass.  Fgravity = (m) x (9.8 m/s2) 

 

Dependent Var. ----> 8.  Knowing your unbalanced force from #6, and the force of gravity acting on your mass from #7, determine the force your wind machine exerts on the mass.  Fwindmachine – Fgravity = Funbalanced

 

9.  Determine the total height the mass is lifted.  hfinal – hinitial = distance

 

Dependent Var. ----> 10.  Calculate the work the wind machine does on the mass in lifting it to the total height you found in #9.  Remember, physical work = (force) x (displacement).

 

11.  Calculate the work gravity does on the mass in lifting it to the total height.  Remember,

 W = (force) x (displacement).

 

12.  Calculate the gravitational potential energy of the mass after it is at its total height.  Remember that gravitational potential energy = (mass) x (9.8 m/s2) x (total height)

 

13.  How do your answers for #11 and #12 compare?

 

14.  Calculate the kinetic energy of the mass when it is on the ground.  Remember, kinetic energy = (1/2)x(mass)x(speed)2

 

15.  Calculate the kinetic energy of the mass when it is at its final height.

 

16.  What is the net work done on the mass?  Wwindmachine – Wgravity = Wtotal

 

17.  How do your answers to #16 and #15 compare?

 

Dependent Var. ----> 18.  Calculate the power exerted by your wind machine using your answer to #10 and the time required to lift the mass.  Remember, power = (Wwindmachine) / (elapsed time).  Give your answer in watts.
