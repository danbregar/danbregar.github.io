Force Diagrams

Physical Science and Technology

 

Using your own paper, draw a force diagram for Mr. Bregar in questions 1-5.  Show all forces acting ON Mr. Bregar.  Do not show forces that Mr. Bregar is exerting. 

 

Remember:

 - You should only draw one object!

 - The length of the line should indicate its strength

 - The direction of the line should be in the direction of the force (use an arrow pointing away from the object for a pulling force; use an arrow pointing towards the object for a pushing force)

 - You should label all the forces with the cause of the force and whether it is a push or a pull

 - Include the following forces:

⁭   Any forces described in the problem as pushing or pulling on the object

⁭   Gravity

⁭   Friction

⁭   Supporting Forces

⁭   Newton’s 3rd Law Forces* (we will discuss this later in the week)

 

1.   Mr. Bregar is being stepped upon by an elephant.

 

 

2.   Mr. Bregar is in outer space and a Martian is pushing him.  (Use a squiggly line to indicate if you think there will be a net, or unbalanced, force on Mr. Bregar and in what direction it will be.)

 

 

3.   Mr. Bregar is being squished by a vise (he is being suspended above the floor, held horizontally in the vise).

 

 

4.   Mr. Bregar is being pulled in one direction by Vanessa Rencehausen and in the opposite direction by Trent Jones.  Vanessa is pulling twice as hard as Trent is.  Mr. Bregar is sliding along the floor towards Vanessa.

 

 

5.   Mr. Bregar is floating in outer space, pushing on a rocket.
