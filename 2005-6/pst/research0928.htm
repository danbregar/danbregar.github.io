Research Questions for Semester Project

Physical Science and Technology

 

Now that you have found something interesting that you would like to pursue for your semester project, it is time to start coming up with ideas for a specific question that you can ask and answer in this class.  In order to conduct your study, you will need to take measurements using scientific instruments.  Today, you will be brainstorming some specific measurements you could take that relate to your project idea.

 

Here are some examples of the types of instruments we have here at CVHS that you could use in your study:

·        Ruler

·        Motion detector (can measure speed, distance and acceleration)

·        Voltmeter

·        Pressure sensor

·        pH sensor

·        Temperature sensor

·        Light sensor

·        Thermometer

·        3-beam balance

·        Magnetic sensor

·        Force probe

·        Conductivity probe

·        Microphone (can measure volume or pitch of sound)

 

Some of these instruments are designed to work alone; others are used with a computer or another device.

 

You have two goals for today.  Please record this information by hand onto a separate sheet of paper to turn in today.

1.   Come up with at least three practical measurements you could make that relate to your project idea.  For example, if you are interested in guitars, you could measure the volume using a microphone, the speed of a vibrating string using a motion detector, or the pressure changes of the top of the guitar using a pressure sensor.

 

2.   For each of the measurements you thought of in step 1, try to find a web site that describes how someone else took this measurement.  Once you find a web site, write down the URL (web address) and a one or two sentence summary of the measurements the web site describes.  For example, this physics class site http://www.glenbrook.k12.il.us/gbssci/phys/class/sound/u11l2a.html talks about how a pressure sensor could be used to measure the vibration rate of a guitar string, since the vibration of the string produces a pressure wave (sound wave) in the air.
