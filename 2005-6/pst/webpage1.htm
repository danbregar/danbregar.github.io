Web Page Portfolio Assignment

Physical Science & Technology

 

In this class, you will create and post a web page on the Internet that will serve as an online portfolio of your work throughout the semester.  This will also eventually become a primary communication tool with respect to upcoming projects.  Think of your web page as the means by which you are going to show the world what you have learned in this class. 

 

For this web page you are to do the following:

1.      Use Microsoft FrontPage to create your main page.  Save your main page as index.htm in a folder called “PST Web Page” on your H: drive.  This will be the page people first go to when accessing your materials.  All of your other materials will be linked here.

2.      On your index page, create a place for the following (this place could be other web pages if you wish):

a)      Completed assignments

b)      Related Links to other web sites.

c)      Personal information about your interests.  Include some pictures, but remember to cite your source!

d)      Include a link to the CV Science Department Web Page:  http://www2.corvallis.k12.or.us/cvhs/science/index.htm .

e)      Include a link to the class web page:  http://www2.corvallis.k12.or.us/cvhs/bregard/pst.htm .

f)        Copy your Inflatable Structure project spreadsheet into your “PST Web Page” folder so it will be easily accessible from your index page

g)      Create a link to your Inflatable Structure project spreadsheet.

3.      When you know your web page is working, let Mr. Bregar or Mr. Baldwin know so we can check your work.

4.      We have not yet determined where your web pages will ultimately be located – we may have you create a web site on Geocities, or we may have access to a web server here at the school.  If you already have a web page, and know how to upload your PST web page, you may do that.  However, you are not required to have your web page on line at this time.

5.      You will be given time periodically to work on your web pages in class.  However, some work may be required outside of class as the year proceeds.

6.      Your web pages will be periodically checked and graded throughout the semester.
