Power Problems

Physical Science and Technology

 

Use your own paper to answer the following questions.  Show the Five Steps where indicated.

 

1.   What is the definition of power (NOT the formula - the definition!)?

 

2.   What is the formula for power?

 

3.   What are the units of power?

 

4.   In order for the equations and units above to be calculated properly, you need to make sure that you have made any measurements of time, distance, and mass into the proper units.  What are the appropriate units of:

a.   Time: _______________

b.   Distance: _____________

c.   Mass: ______________

 

5.   If a frog pulls a car for 17.6 m with a force of 17.8 Newtons for 7 seconds, then:

a)   How much work did the frog do (use the Five Steps)?

b)   How much power was the frog exerting (use the Five Steps)?

 

6.   If a cat pushes a bike over a distance of 7 meters with a force of 87.6 Newtons for 4.88 seconds, then:

a)   How much work did the cat do (use the Five Steps)?

b)   How much power was the cat exerting (use the Five Steps)?

 

7.   How much work can a bunny do in 17.2 seconds if it can generate 992.4 Watts of power (Use the Five Steps)?

 

8.   A monkey riding a unicycle can produce about 44.8 Watts of power.  If the monkey rides for 246 seconds, how much work did it do (Use the Five Steps)?

 

9.   A chicken can generate 432.54 Watts of power.  If the chicken runs for 79 seconds and travels 321 m, then:

a)   How much work did the chicken do (use the Five Steps)?

b)   How much force was the chicken exerting (use the Five Steps)?

 

10. A mosquito can generate 0.0194 Watts of power. If it takes the mosquito 0.56 Newtons to lift a small piece of cheese 773.4 meters then:

a)   How much work did the mosquito do (use the Five Steps)?

b)   How much time did it take the mosquito to lift the cheese (use the Five Steps)?
