1.   Scooter is driving down 9th Street in his 1962 Corvette.  He drives 120 meters in 17 seconds.  Assuming that he does not speed up or slow down, what is his speed in meters per second?

 

2.   Liesel Kunz is running along a track that is 251 meters long.  It takes her 12.6 seconds to run the entire length of the track.  What is her speed in meters per second?

 

3.   Mr. Bregar fell out of a plane.  He fell 1,982 meters in 3 seconds.  What was his average speed in meters per second?

 

4.   Mr. Baldwin is walking to a friend’s house.  He walks 0.75 meters north, then realizes he walked too far.  He turns around and walks 0.25 meters south.  The entire walk takes him 13 seconds.  What was his speed in meters per second?

 

5.   In problem #4, what was Mr. Baldwin’s velocity in meters per second?

 

6.   Daniel Rosenberg is running around in a circle.  The circle is 2500 meters long.  He is running at a constant speed.  It takes him 452 seconds to complete the circle and get back to where he started.  What is Daniel’s speed in meters per second?

 

7.   In problem #6, what is Daniel’s velocity in meters per second?

 

8.   Sarah Hubbard is running down the street at 7.5 meters per second.  She sees a double-tall latte on the road ahead of her and starts sprinting.  After 3 seconds have passed, Sarah is running at 9.1 meters per second.  What was Sarah’s acceleration?

 

9.   Trent Jones’s older brother Grant is falling off a cliff.  At the beginning of his fall, his velocity is 0 meters per second.  After 10 seconds, his velocity is 283 meters per second.  What was Grant’s acceleration?  (Grant was uninjured when he hit the ground because he landed on Mr. Bregar).

 

10. A go-cart is rolling around a track at 6.5 miles per hour.  The track is circular.  The go-cart’s speed does not change.  Is the go-cart accelerating?  Explain why or why not.   P.S.  Mr. Bregar is being dragged behind the cart.  (The Five Steps are not necessary to solve this problem)
