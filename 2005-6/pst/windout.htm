WIND MACHINE PROJECT

HARNESSING ENERGY FROM THE WIND

Physical Science & Technology

 

Producing energy from the wind is an old idea that is gaining new interest around the world.  Some are suggesting that harnessing the power of the wind could become a major means of generating electricity in the very near future.  For the next three weeks in this class, you will be working in pairs to build and test your own wind machine.  You will have opportunities to test your machine in a wind tunnel.  This project will help you understand the physics involved in both wind machine design and concepts of motion, force, work, energy, and power.

 

The Project

  1. Design and construct a wind machine that can lift 100.0 grams a minimum of 0.5 meters.
  2. After performance Trial#1 of your initial design, you will select one characteristic of your wind machine to change in attempting to improve your wind machine’s performance.
  3. Change this characteristic and measure the performance of your modified design during performance Trial #2.
  4. Change this characteristic a second time and measure the performance of your modified design during performance Trial #3.
  5. After each trial, use measured data to complete some calculations associated with motion, force, and energy.
  6. Complete a report on your entire process.  This report will be described later and, should you do a good job, may satisfy your Science Inquiry work sample for your CIM.

 

The requirements for the wind machines are as follows

  1. No “kits” may be used.  Only materials provided in class or brought from home may be used to build the machine.
  2. The total diameter cut by the blades of the wind machine may be no more than 28.0 cm. (This will be measured from the tip of one blade to a point 180 degrees from that blade tip.)
  3. Each wind machine must lift 100.0 grams a distance of 0.5 meters.
  4. Students are to work in pairs.
  5. Students will be provided with a piece of welding rod to be used for shafting.

 

Two main criteria will be used to evaluate your wind machine

  1. Wind machine design: Does your machine consistently and safely lift 100.0 grams at least 0.5 meters?
  2. Experimental design:  Has your group produced a valid experimental design to test and improve your wind machine?  (This part of the project will include a write up, some measurements, and some calculations.  More details on this stuff will be provided later).
