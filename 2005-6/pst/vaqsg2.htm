Study Guide #2 – Velocity and Acceleration Quiz

Physical Science and Technology

 

Instructions:  Please complete these problems on your own sheet of paper – write your name, today’s date, and the due date (today) in the top left corner of your paper.  Write the title of this assignment (“SG 2 – Velocity and Acceleration Quiz”) across the top of your page.  Use the Five Steps where indicated.

 

1.   A turtle is running down the street with a velocity of 11.45 m/s.  If it accelerates at 1.3 m/s for 16.3 s, what will its velocity be?  Use the Five Steps)

 

2.   A gigantic ant is running through the hallways of a spaceship.  If it runs for 11.8 seconds, starts with a velocity of 7.2 m/s, and accelerates at 8.9 m/s2, what will its final velocity be?  Use the Five Steps.

 

3.   A ferret is walking with a velocity of 7.2 m/s.  If it travels 78.2 m, for how long was it walking?  Use the Five Steps.

 

4.   Mr. Bregar is running around the earth at the equator.  If he runs for 6,824,342,111.2342 seconds, accelerates at 7.47238742971823 m/s2, and ends up in the exact same spot he started, what was his final velocity?  Use the Five Steps.  (Hint – what was Mr. Bregar’s displacement?)

 

5.   A bear is digging into the ground, tunneling after Mr. Bregar.  After 145.8 seconds of digging, his velocity is 2.187 m/s.  If he was accelerating at 0.015 m/s/s while he was running, what was his original velocity?  Use the Five Steps.

 

6.   After the bear catches and eats Mr. Bregar, it decides to take a nap.  So it walks to its den, which is 4,521.8 meters away from the picnic table where it ate Mr. Bregar.  If it takes the bear 77.8 seconds to get to its den, what was its velocity?  Use the Five Steps.
