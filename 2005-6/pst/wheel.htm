Reinventing the Wheel

Physical Science and Technology

 

Introduction:

The purpose of this activity is for you to demonstrate your experience in designing and building a device, using an Excel spreadsheet to make calculations and predictions, and writing the results in a formal lab paper.  Each section of this activity has multiple criteria; please finish as many criteria as you can in the time allowed.  You will work individually to complete this activity, but you are encouraged to discuss ideas with and seek help from your peers.  We will divide the class in half and each half will work on one section at a time (the sections can be done in any order).

 

Thursday, September 9

à  Build the wheel

Each of you will be assigned two numbers.  The first number will represent a distance and the second will represent a number of rotations.  Your goal will be to build a wheel that will travel the specified distance in the specified number of rotations.  A variety of materials will be provided to you.

Criteria 1:   Determine your distance and rotation using the formula described in class (# of letters in last name = # of rotations; # of letters in first name * # of letters in last name * 4 = distance; Group 1 = inches; Group 2 = cm)

Criteria 2:   Build a wheel that travels the specified distance in the specified number of rotations

Criteria 3:   The distance traveled should be within 5% of the target distance for at least 5 separate trials

Criteria 4:   The wheel should have a hole exactly in the center for an axle (such as a ballpoint pen)

Criteria 5:   The wheel should be perfectly round

 

à Make a data and calculation table

You will use the computers in the drafting lab to make a Microsoft Excel spreadsheet that will allow you to display, calculate, and predict information related to this activity.  For this section, you will need to work in pairs.

Criteria 1:   Make a data table that shows the distance traveled and rotations made by your wheel for 5-10 trials (if you haven’t collected the data yet, leave the entries blank)

Criteria 2:   Make sure your data table is properly labeled and includes correct units

Criteria 3:   Make an additional table in Excel that allows you to type in a distance in centimeters and a rotation and use an Excel formula to predict the correct size of wheel to make

Criteria 4:   Make sure your prediction table is properly labeled and includes correct units

Criteria 5:   Add a column to your prediction table that allows you to type in a distance in inches instead of centimeters

 

Friday, September 10

à  Make a web page for this activity

You will use Microsoft FrontPage on the laptops to make a web page that describes this activity

Criteria 1:   You should have a main page that shows your name, the date, the period, the name of the class, and a title.  This page should link to all other pages, and all the other pages should be able to link back to this one.

Criteria 2:   Include an introduction slide that explains the purpose of this activity – include your target distance and number of rotations

Criteria 3:   Include a data table, made from within FrontPage that shows the distance traveled and number of rotations made by your wheel in each trial.

Criteria 4:   Include a page of images (from the internet) of wheels, rulers, and mathematical formulas

Criteria 5:   Include a movie (from the internet) that shows a rolling object

Criteria 6:   Make sure all your pages are attractively formatted with a similar theme

 

à  Write a lab report documenting your progress

You will use Microsoft Word on the computers in the science department computer lab

Criteria 1:   Your lab report should have your name, the period, and the date in the upper right-hand corner

Criteria 2:   Include a title centered and in bold

Criteria 3:   Include an Introduction section describing the purpose of this activity and what you learned

Criteria 4:   Include a Methods section that is a detailed list of all the steps you followed in order to build your wheel and how you collected your data

Criteria 5:   Include a data table, made from within Word, that shows the distance traveled and number of rotations made by your wheel in each trial.  Make sure the table is appropriately labeled and attractively formatted

Criteria 6:   Include a Discussion section that addresses the following questions:

1.      What aspects of science interest you the most?

2.      What aspects of technology interest you the most?

3.      Do you use computers at home?  If so, what do you use them for?

4.      Do you have or have you ever used wood or metal working tools?  If so, what types of things have you made or repaired?

5.      Do you have any comments, suggestions, or questions for the instructors?
