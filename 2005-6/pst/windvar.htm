SCIENTIFIC INVESTIGATIONS

INDEPENDENT VS. DEPENDENT VARIABLES

Physical Science & Technology

 

To produce electricity, windmills convert the energy of the wind into electrical energy.  To do this efficiently, the windmill needs to be designed to use as much of the wind energy as possible.  Windmill design and the materials used have changed over the years to do just this.  To improve design, researchers choose a variable to change (the independent variable) that they think may improve the efficiency of the windmill, and then test the effect of changing the independent variable on a second variable (the dependent variable).  This approach should sound familiar to you, and it is the one that you will use for this project.

 

Independent Variables

Following your first test of the wind machine, you will choose an independent variable to test.  There are many possibilities for this.  For example, you may change the pitch of the blades, or the materials used, or any other change that you think may improve the performance of your wind machine.  Be sure to change only one variable at a time.

 

Constants

When you wish to see how changing one variable (your independent variable above) impacts the performance of your wind machine, you want to keep all other variable quantities the same.  For instance, if you wish to test how the number of blades impact the performance of your wind machine, you must keep variables such as blade size, angle, shape, material, etc… the same.  These variables that are kept constant during each of your trials are known as your constants.

 

Dependent variables

You will test the ability of your wind machine to perform several tasks.

 

1.      The maximum speed attained by your wind machine as it rotates in the wind tunnel without a weight attached.

2.      The maximum weight lifted by the machine.

3.      Whether your machine is able to lift 100 grams to a height of 0.5 meters

 

The ability of your machine to perform these tasks defines for you the possible dependent variables that you will be measuring.  Also related to each of these tasks are specific measurements and calculations that can be made to test the impact of your modifications.  These measurements and calculated values are to be reported in standard units, and are to be included in you final report.

 

1.      Measure the blade speed using a strobe light.

2.      Measure the maximum weight lifted by the machine and the height to which this weight is lifted.  Calculate the work done by the machine for this mass.

3.      Measure the time required for the machine to lift 100 g to a height of 0.5 m.  Calculate the force and the power exerted by the machine.
