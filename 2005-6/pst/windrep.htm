Wind Machine Project Write-Up

 

 

Each person must turn in a word-processed write-up that includes each of the following four sections.  Each of these parts, as well as your final write-up, will be posted on your website.

 

TITLE AND INTRODUCTION

This section must include:

a)     An explanation.  Why is the investigation being carried out? 

b)     Background information. Why do we care about wind power?  What are its advantages?  What are its disadvantages?  Include a brief history, with examples, of how the wind has been harnessed in the past?  How does the process you use in this investigation compare with past activities? 

c)      A brief summary of your investigation.  Here you will explain your investigation and why your group has chosen to investigate the variable that you have selected.

 

METHODS

This section must include:

a)     All procedures and methods you utilize throughout your entire investigation including the reason you are using them.  What you place in this section should be detailed enough so that another person who is reading your procedure can carry out your procedure without having to ask you any questions.  Pictures, diagrams, and sketches work great here.

b)     A list of all materials utilized.

c)      A list of your independent and dependent variables (you may have more than one dependent variable), your controls, and your constants.

d)     A list of all assumptions you make.

 

RESULTS

This section must include:

a)     Tables of data and measurements.

b)     Calculations to determine the dependent variables (these will be identified on a separate document).

c)      Minimize your discussion here – your commentary belongs elsewhere.

 

DISCUSSION

This section must respond to the following:

a)     What do your results mean?  Based upon your results, do you have any recommendations for designers of wind machines?

b)     Based upon your investigation, what are the next things you might want to investigate in further research?  What should someone else look into?

c)      Make a connection between your results and implications to our larger society.
