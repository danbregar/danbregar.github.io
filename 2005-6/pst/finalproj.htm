Final Project for Garfield School

Physical Science and Technology

 

Purpose:  To create an interactive, technology-based activity that is portable so we can take it to Garfield Elementary School and have Garfield students learn the physical science associated with the technology.

 

Your specific goal will be to create a laboratory-type activity that will allow someone to manipulate or adjust a device and take measurements in order to find out how the adjustment affected the device’s operation.  The measurements that you will take will be force measurements using one of the Vernier Force Sensors.  With this information, you will be able to calculate additional physics quantities such as work, power, and energy.  An example would be an adjustable ramp with a force sensor attached to the bottom of it – someone could adjust the height of the ramp, roll an object down the ramp, and then measure the force of the object when it hits the force sensor.  This project would allow someone to answer the question “How does the height (slope) of a ramp relate to the force of an object rolling down the ramp?”.  Your project should allow someone to answer a similar question.

 

Step 1:  Develop a question that your device will be designed to answer.  Have your question approved by an instructor.  Target date:  Friday, 4/21/06

 

Step 2:  Make 10 thumbnail sketches for your device.  Target date:  Friday, 4/21/06

 

Step 3:  Find two other people in the class who are interested in a device similar to yours.  Have your group members approved by an instructor.  Target date:  Tuesday, 4/25/06

 

Step 4:  Pick one device for your group to build, test, and demonstrate.  Make a quick full-page sketch of your final idea and have it approved by an instructor.  Target date:  Tuesday, 4/25/06

 

Step 5:  Make a set of scale orthographic drawings for your device (front, side, top).  Target Date:  Friday, 4/28/06

 

Step 6a (at the same time as step 6b):  Build and test your device.  Target date:  Thursday, 6/1/06

 

Step 6b (at the same time as step 6a):  Make a web page that will provide instructions for using the device, guidelines for using the measuring tools, and questions that will help other students conduct an investigation of their own design.  (More info will be given later.)  Target date:  Thursday, 6/1/06

 

Step 7:  Demonstrate your device and let other students test it.  Due date:  Friday, 6/2/06

 

Students whose projects work reliably will be visiting Garfield Elementary between Tuesday, 6/6/06 and Tuesday, 6/13/06.
