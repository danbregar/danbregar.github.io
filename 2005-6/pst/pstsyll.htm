Guidelines and Expectations

Physical Science & Technology

 

 

Welcome to Physical Science & Technology!  In this course expect to utilize computer tools and other technology applications to increase your understanding of physical science.  Class activities will focus on mastering concepts of measurement, motion, force, work and energy, the atom, and, depending on student progress, some basic chemistry.  Students will receive instruction on basic programming, web page construction, and other technology tools.  The course has a significant lab aspect in which students construct objects.  Expect to couple these tools of technology with your understanding of science in order to complete numerous projects.

 

Expectations:

  1. Demonstrate appropriate behavior (see back) and use appropriate language at all times.
  2. Come to class every day and on time - three tardies will result in disciplinary action, and ten unexcused absences may result in denial of credit.
  3. Be positive and prepared.
  4. Bring your binder to all classes every day.
  5. If you have a question about your grades, please talk with us after class. (Grades will be posted on line - please check them, as we are not perfect).

 

Work Policy:

Your grade will be based upon the following:

  1. Daily Work - due in the basket by 3 PM on the due date. If it comes in one day late, it’s 30% off (please have an instructor sign your paper to verify that it was only one day late).  Work turned in more than one day late will not receive credit. If you had an excused absence, and do not deserve the penalty, please have an instructor sign the paper to verify that it is not late.
  2. Journals – collected and graded at the end of every other week.  A complete journal, with Table of Contents, will receive full credit. If you are absent you may turn your notebook in the next week for full credit.
  3. Tests/Quizzes - retakes are allowed only for excused absences.  If you skip during a test or quiz, you cannot make up the work.
  4. Projects – need to be turned in on time; no excuses.
  5. Participation – participation points will be assigned to students who attend class daily, arrive on time,  behave in a safe and responsible manner, and contribute to their own and others’ learning.

 

Late work:

  1. “Students are responsible for making up any school work missed during an ‘excused’ absence.  Arrangements for late assignments should be made with the teacher(s) immediately upon the student’s return to school…” (from the CVHS Student Handbook)
  2. All late work must be made up within two weeks of the student’s return to school or the student will receive a score of 0 (unless a special arrangement is made with the teacher).
  3. All make-up work must be turned in before the last week of the quarter in order to receive a grade!
  4. FOR A STUDENT TO RECEIVE CREDIT IN PHYSICAL SCIENCE & TECHNOLOGY, ALL ASSIGNMENTS MUST BE TURNED IN.  Assignments that are missed due to unexcused absences or are more than one day late will receive a score of 0, but must still be turned in (if the assignment is incomplete or deemed to be of particularly poor quality, the student will be asked to redo and resubmit it).  Students who have a grade of “D” or above will receive a “G” (incomplete) on their report cards if they are missing assignments.  Students who have a grade of “F” will receive an “F” on their report card regardless of whether or not they are missing assignments.

 

Appropriate Behavior

Physical Science & Technology

 

For this part of your day, being a Physical Science & Technology student is your job.  As with any other job, you are expected to be respectful, responsible, and safe at all times. 

 

1.  Respect

·   Understand and demonstrate self-respect: be honest with, care about, and take responsibility for yourself.

·   Accept others as you would want to be accepted.

·   Communicate (verbally and non-verbally) in ways that do not annoy or humiliate others.

·   Use equipment correctly.

·   Give other students the opportunity to learn at their maximum potential.

·   Cooperate with each other and with staff members.

·   Demonstrate a positive attitude towards staff members and students.

 

2.  Responsibility

·   Attend class regularly and promptly.

·   Seek and complete make-up work.

·   Help clean the materials and shop area (and other rooms as needed).

·   Ask for help.

·   Work cooperatively with others.

·   Support other students’ learning.

·   Use your time wisely.

 

3.  Safety

·   Please respect the physical and emotional safety of others in this class.

·   Please refer to the Safety Contracts for additional safety requirements in the Materials Lab and classrooms.
