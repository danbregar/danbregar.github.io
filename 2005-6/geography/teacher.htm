Where in the World

A Geography Activity

 

Grade Level: K – 6

 

Objectives: 

a.   Students will identify the different nationalities represented by the people who work on the Resolution.

b.   Students will locate different countries on a world map.

c.   Students will examine some of the advantages and difficulties of working with a multinational crew.

 

Materials:

  • Computers with web access and speakers, or one computer with a projector and speakers.
  • World map printout

 

Additional Teacher Information:

A challenging but fun extension to this activity would be to have students try to translate some of the videos.  The success of this would obviously depend on the resources students have available.  One possible way to solve this problem would be to e-mail the videos to teachers at schools in the other countries to have their students help with the translations.

 

The company that contracts the ship’s operating crew and technicians is called Transocean.  The company that contracts the “hotel” staff (the people who run the galley and maintain the cabins) is called Catamar.  Both of these companies have information on their web sites that could provide additional resources for the discussion questions.

 

Possible responses to discussion question #1 – to represent the different member countries in IODP.  Because different people have different skills – and the best people for a job might not come from the U.S.

 

Possible responses to discussion question #2 – people might have trouble understanding each other in certain situations.  To solve this problem, signs and information might be posted in multiple languages, or certain people could serve as translators for others.

 

Possible responses to discussion question #3 – they fly or drive to the ports where the ship is located.  The people who operate the ship organize it- there are probably several people who work full-time just arranging travel for all the people who need to go to ships or go home from ships.

 

Possible responses to discussion question #4 – look up in the information in an encyclopedia or on the Internet.  Find someone who speaks that language and have them help translate.  E-mail the crew members on the JR and ask them to translate themselves!
