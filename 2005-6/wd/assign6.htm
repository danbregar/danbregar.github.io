Assignment #6

Web Design

 

The purpose of this assignment is to give you a chance to experiment with frames and to allow you to expand your personal web site so that it has more than one page in it.

 

Frames allow you to have a standard set of navigation links and a standard banner on your web site, so if your navigation links or banner ever changes, you’ll only have to change it once for the whole site, not once for every page.  To add frames to your home page, do the following:

 

1.   Create a new folder in your WD folder on your H: drive – call it “Frames”.

2.   Open up your personal web page index.htm file – choose “Save As” from the “File” menu and save it to your “Frames” folder with the name “main.htm”.

3.   Select “New” from the file menu.  On the right-hand side of the screen, choose “More Page Templates”, then click on the “Frames” tab of the window that appears.  Choose any style of frames you want.

4.   When the new frames page appears on your screen, choose “Set Initial Page” from one of the blank areas and navigate to the “main.htm” file you just saved.

5.   Save your new file as “index.htm” inside the “Frames” folder.

 

Now, you can add menu items and/or graphics to the other frames on your page.  You can do this by using “Set Initial Page” and choosing a page you’ve already created, or you can make new content right on the new screen.

 

For this assignment, add some navigation links to your personal home page.  Each link should go to a different web page – at least two of these pages should be pages that you create on your own; you can also add links to external web sites.  To make these pages, you can choose “Save As” from the “File” menu and save your index.htm file with a new name.  Then, you can change the main frame of the page – the text, links, and graphics in the other frames will stay the same, but if you change them in any of your pages, they will change in all of your pages.  Here are some suggested additional pages for your personal web site:

-     Your activities and interests

-     Your class information and academic interests

-     Information about your friends and family
