Assignment #14 – Community Web Site Preview

Web Design

 

The next step in creating your community web site is to begin adding information, pictures, links, and buttons to your documents.  Since each of you is responsible for two pages of the web site, the way you should create these “preview pages” is for each of you, independently, to create one of your pages.  Then, as a group, you can review all of these pages, pick the characteristics that you like the best, and use those as the defining guidelines for your entire web site.

 

Today, each of you should create one preview page.  Make sure that:

1.   Your preview page uses the correct layout, font, and color scheme

2.   Your preview page includes text and graphics

3.   Your preview page has hover buttons as a site menu

4.   Your preview page uses inline frames to separate various parts of the page

5.   Your preview page uses tables within each section to organize graphics and text

 

Try to make an effort to have the visual elements of your page line up well – for example, you can pair blocks of text with small graphics in order to create a sense of balance on your page.  Also, you can make sure that the hover buttons for your menu extend all the way across or down the page (depending on how you orient them).

 

On Thursday, you and your group will evaluate each other’s preview pages in order to determine the final characteristics of your web site.
