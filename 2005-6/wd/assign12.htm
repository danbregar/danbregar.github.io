Assignment #12 – Community Web Site

Web Design

 

Your next project in Web Design will be to create a web site for an organization in the community.  This organization can be one that you are part of, but it does not have to be.  The organization should be one that either does not have a web page or would like to redesign an existing web page.

 

For this assignment, I would like you to use a new types of control in Microsoft FrontPage:  “Hover Buttons”.  Hover buttons are located in the Insert Menu – choose “Interactive Button”.  There are a list of options you can experiment with.  You should use the hover buttons in place of text or graphic hyperlinks WITHIN your web site.  Examples of hover buttons and pull-down menus can be seen on the new Corvallis School District web page (http://www.csd509j.net).  The main links on the page are hover buttons – they change color as the mouse moves over the top of them.

 

For this web site, you should use in-line frames rather than regular frames – in-line frames can be inserted from the Insert Menu – choose “Inline Frame”.  Inline frames work almost the same way as regular frames, but they are a little easier to manipulate and edit.  If you right-click on an in-line frame and choose “In-line Frame Properties”, you can change the appearance of the frame.

 

This new web site should have at least two pages for each person in your group – so each of you will be responsible for creating two complete web pages that will be part of the web site.

 

You should work in a group with one or two other people for this assignment and project.  Here are the steps you should complete today:

 

1.   Choose your partners

2.   On a sheet of paper, write your name and the names of your partners.

3.   On your paper, make a list of five different organizations you think you would like to make a web site for.

4.   On your paper, describe / draw at least three different layouts that you could use for this web site.  Include information about color schemes, fonts, location of images / graphics, and the location of menus, buttons, and hyperlinks.

5.   On your paper, describe the information you will include on each of the pages that would make up the web site, along with who would be responsible for these pages.  You do not need to do this for all five of the organizations you have picked – but you should do it for at least two (pick the two that you are most interested in and you feel you are most likely to be able to do).

6.   With your partners, begin creating a “template” page – a web page that has the right color scheme, font scheme, and layouts so that you can all make web pages that have the same look and feel.

7.   Turn in your paper to the instructor at the end of the period.
