Assignment #8

Web Design

 

The purpose of this activity is to help you to understand and create image maps by using hot spots.  An image map is a picture that is broken into different regions, each of which is a hyperlink.  These regions are called hot spots.

 

To create an image map, follow these steps:

1.   Insert a new picture into your web page.

2.   Select this picture by clicking on it.

3.   From the “View” menu, choose “Toolbars” and select “Pictures”.

4.   At the far right of the Pictures toolbar, there are three icons for drawing hotspots – rectangular, circular, and polygonal.  Experiment with each of these by drawing on your picture to discover how they work (hint – for polygonal hotspots, click once to create a line break and double-click to finish drawing).  After you have drawn a hotspot, a hyperlink dialog box will appear, letting you choose the appropriate link.

5.   To change a hotspot after you’ve drawn it, click on one of the square handles and drag.

 

For this assignment, you should add an image map to two of the pages in your final personal web site.  The links can be to pages within your web site or to external web sites.
