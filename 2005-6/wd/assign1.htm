Web Design

Assignment #1

 

The purpose of this assignment is two-fold:  to give you an opportunity to look at existing public web pages with an eye for content and design, and to give you some experience working with Microsoft FrontPage as a tool for creating web content.

 

Part A:

Visit and explore the following websites (spend about 5 minutes or so looking at each site):

http://www.microsoft.com

http://www.msnbc.com

http://news.google.com

http://www.csd509j.net/cvhs

 

For each site, write out short answers (1 – 2 sentences) to the following questions:

1.   How would you describe the physical arrangement of the website into menus, contents, images, etc.?

2.   Do you think it is easy to find information on the site, or is it difficult to figure out how things work?

3.   How does the color scheme and font choice affect your ability to navigate around the site?

4.   Do features such as menus, animation, and ads make it easier or more difficult for you to use the site?

5.   What types of things might you change about the site if you were the designer?

Write your name on your paper and turn it in to the instructor.

 

Part B:

Open up Microsoft FrontPage on your computer.  Create a document that might serve as the foundation for your personal web page.  Include information such as your age, year in school, academic and social interests, hobbies and activities, etc.  You do not need to put hyperlinks or pictures in this document (but you can if you want).  Use the toolbar in FrontPage to set the fonts, colors, highlights, and other formatting options.  Save this document to your H: drive, in a folder called “MyWebPage”, as “index.htm”.  We will review and expand this document in class tomorrow.

 

Part C: 

(** only for students enrolled in Web Design without being enrolled in Visual Basic **)

Make a full-page (8.5” x 11”) sketch of five different home pages that you could create in this class:

1.   A personal home page for you.

2.   A home page for your family or friends.

3.   A home page for one of your teachers.

4.   A home page for Crescent Valley High School.

5.   A home page for an activity you are involved in.

You can use your own ideas for how the page should look, or you can get ideas from other web pages.  Each sketch should include the following information:

a)   The location of menu items and links

b)   The location and type of images that should appear

c)   A summary of the content (information) that will appear in the different sections of the page

d)   Any stylistic information such as font sizes, styles, or color schemes

 

Make sure your name is on your sketches and turn them in to the instructor.
