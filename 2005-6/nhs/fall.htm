National Honor Society – Liberty Chapter

Member Information – 2004-2005

 

Welcome back to the National Honor Society!

 

Dues:

Those of you who were initiated prior to April 2004 and have not paid your outstanding dues from last year are no longer in NHS.

 

This year’s dues will be $5 for all new and current members.  Members who were initiated last spring need but have not yet paid dues need to pay an additional $5 fee ($10 total).

 

Dues will be accepted through December 17 without penalty; after December 17, there will be an additional $20 fee ($25 total).  If you have not paid your dues by May 27, you will be dropped from NHS.

 

Please make your checks out to National Honor Society and give them to Mr. Bregar in F-18.

 

Service:

Members of NHS need to participate in a minimum of 10 hours of community service per semester.  You should log your hours in the service logbook in Mr. Bregar’s room (F-18).  If you do not fulfill your service commitment for this year, you will be dropped from NHS.  Examples of services projects are Stone Soup, Habitat for Humanity, volunteering to baby-sit at your church, helping out at CV Open House, serving at the annual AFS dessert – anything that is unpaid volunteer work not required by another class.  If you need suggestions or have a question about service, please talk to Mr. Bregar.

 

Yearly Project:

Each year, NHS holds a mandatory service project for all members.  Not participating in the project will result in a $10.00 fine.  This year’s project will be held in April.  Please see Mr. Bregar if you have any suggestions about what we could do for the project.

 

Grades:

If your overall GPS drops below 3.33, you will be notified that you are on probation.  Once on probation, you have one semester in which to raise your GPA to 3.33 or you will be dropped from NHS.

 

Leadership and Activities:

You were chosen to be part of NHS because of your academic abilities, character, and leadership.  If you have ideas for projects or activities for NHS or for Crescent Valley High School, please talk to Mr. Bregar or to the NHS officers.  It is my hope that you will use this opportunity to get involved with your school and your community.

 

Officers:

If you are interested in being on the NHS Officer Board, please talk to Mr. Bregar.  Officers are responsible for coordinating school-wide NHS activities, reviewing prospective members, and attending monthly meetings with Mr. Bregar.
