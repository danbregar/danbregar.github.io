Project Poster Guidelines

Advanced Field Biology

 

Here are the requirements for the posters that you create that will be displayed in the science wing at CV in order to showcase your projects.  They are due on Wednesday, June 15.  You should include lots of pictures and maps to make your poster inviting.  The text on your poster should be short (bulleted rather than paragraph format), easy to read from 8-10 feet, and should allow anyone to get an overall impression of your project goals and accomplishments.  Your posters should be visually attractive and contain all of the following information (which is similar to that in your presentations):

 

1.   The question or purpose your group addressed (include enough background information that your viewers can easily comprehend the question/purpose).

2.   A summary of your methods (if your group had several different methods, make sure to clearly indicate which part of your question is addressed by each method).

3.   A summary of your results (include charts, tables, and graphs wherever possible).

4.   A summary of your discussion (again, include charts, tables, and graphs if necessary).
