Background Information:
Orthophosphates
•Phosphorus, the eleventh most abundant mineral on earth, is critical to all life.
•Though phosphates are essential to life, above certain limits they can lead to algal blooms.
•In a freshwater environment, this limit is generally below .1 mg/L.
Nitrates
•Nitrogen and phosphorus levels are directly related, phosphates regulating absorbance of nitrates.
•Overabundance of nitrogen does not become a problem as long as phosphorus remains the limiting factor.
•Acceptable nitrogen levels in a freshwater ecosystem are generally between .1 and 1 mg/L.
