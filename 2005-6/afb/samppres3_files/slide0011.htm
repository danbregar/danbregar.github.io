Analysis of Data
•According to the data we have collected, both Jackson and Frasier Creeks are healthy and have admirable water qualities.
•Macroinvertebrates caught in Frasier Creek were extremely sensitive to pollution, but lacked diversity.
•Jackson Creek had a more diverse macroinvertebrate population, but almost half were pollution tolerant; due to the diversity, these tolerant organisms are acceptable and indicate a healthy stream.
•Normal nitrogen and phosphorous levels were found in both creeks – these results, however, may be flawed. We observed excessive algae in Frazier, yet nitrogen and orthophosphate tests yielded negligible results.
