Background Information:
Macroinvertebrates
•In the Pacific Northwest, there are eight main types of macroinvertebrates. Due to time limitations, we were only able to classify them by order, the most general classification.
•We also determined which orders were sensitive or tolerant to disturbance. If more than 50% of organisms were found to be sensitive, then the overall water quality of the creek would be adequate.
•If fewer than 50% of the macroinvertebrate species were found to be sensitive, that would indicate poor water quality.
