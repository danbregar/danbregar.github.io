Course Syllabus - Advanced Field Biology

Teacher: Dan Bregar

Web Site:  http://web.csd509j.net/cvhs/bregard

 

 

 

Course Theme:   To assess, improve, and restore ecological elements of our community; to use ecological tools to predict and influence the ecological future of Corvallis.

 

Your Goal:   To complete a scientific project and create, as documentation of your work, a written report, a poster, and a PowerPoint presentation.  You will also submit a daily journal, a weekly self-evaluation, and participate in meetings as course assignments.

 

Course Outline:

Key Dates

Brainstorming and group formation

~Feb. 25

Project proposal

Rough draft of proposal due Mar. 18; Final draft due Mar. 31.

Formal data collection and continuing research

Complete by end of April

Data analysis and continuing research

Complete by mid-May

Presentations

Formal run-throughs week of May 16 - 20,

Final presentation week of May 23 - 27 at OSU

Final Report

Rough draft due May 14,

Final draft due June 10

Poster

Final poster due June 10

 

Expectations:   In this class, you will be working largely independently in small groups.  I expect that while you are working on your projects, you will conduct yourself in a professional manner.  You should sign in every day; indicate where you will be and when you will be there.  In addition, each group will meet with me twice each week:  once for a brief logistical meeting and again each Friday for a class meeting of up to 90 minutes. 

 

Grades:             Your grade will be made up as follows:

Proposal                            20%

Presentation                        20%

Final Report                       20%

Poster                                10%

Self-Evaluation                   10%

Journal                               10%

Weekly Meetings                10%

 

 

Notes:              

  • A grade of G (no grade) will be awarded at the end of the 3rd quarter.
  • Upon completion of all assignments, you will be given a grade for both 3rd and 4th quarters (2 credits total).
  • If you withdraw from the class early, you will be given a grade based on the total point value your self-evaluations, your journal, your weekly meetings, and an appropriate percentage of your proposal, final report, presentation, and poster (details for these assignments will be handed out during the 2nd and 3rd weeks of class).
  • Each group will be required to sign a group contract to ensure that conflicts are solved fairly and predictably.
  • Due to budget constraints, the Crescent Valley Science Department will be unable to provide any funds for your projects (you may need to raise funds or apply for a grant).
  • Students must obtain all permits and permissions necessary to conduct their research.
  • Journals must be left in the classroom (F-18) for instructor review at any time.
  • Your group may be required to work with a mentor from the community.  You will need to interact with your mentor in a professional, courteous manner.
  • You should have all phone, e-mail, and written correspondence reviewed by your instructor before you contact your mentor or any involved community members.
  • No late work will be accepted; however, work that is complete and submitted on time can be revised for full credit for up to two weeks after it has been returned to the class.
  • Students who are late, skip class, miss meetings, or fail to report to their field site in a timely manner will be put on an semester-long attendance contract that will be strictly enforced.
  • Students who do not comply with the requirements set forth in this syllabus will be put on a semester-long behavior contract.
