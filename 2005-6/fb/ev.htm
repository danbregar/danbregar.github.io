Evolution Study Questions

Field Biology

 

Instructions:  In small groups, discuss the following questions.  Use the internet to find information about each question.  Make sure that each of you writes answers/notes for each topic.

 

1.   What is science?  What are the “rules” and processes that have to be followed in order for an idea or concept to be scientific?  Can something be true (or correct) without being scientific?

 

2.   What is the scientific definition of evolution?  Is this a fact (something that can be and is observable) or is it a theory?

 

3.   What is natural selection?  How does it work?

 

4.   What is creationism?  How is creationism different from “creation science”?  Is creation science truly a scientific theory?  Why or why not?  (Use your answers for question 1 to help guide your thinking.)

 

5.   Discuss the following statement:  “Evolution … refers to the theory that the first living organism developed from nonliving matter.  Then, as it reproduced, it is said to have changed into different kinds of living things, producing ultimately all forms of life that have ever existed on earth, including humans.”

 

6.   Discuss the following statement:  “…the darker variety of peppered moth, which is said to be a mutant, survived better because it was difficult for birds to see them … the darker variety rapidly became the dominant type.  But was the peppered moth evolving into some other type of insect?  No, it was still exactly the same peppered moth, merely having a different coloration.”
