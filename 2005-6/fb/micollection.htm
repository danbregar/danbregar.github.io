Aquatic Macroinvertebrate Collection Activity

Field Biology

 

To begin with:

Form groups of four, then divide group into two pairs.  Decide which pair will be going outside to collect, and which pair will be setting up the lab station in the room.

 

The inside pair will need to locate a lab station near a sink (lab tables in the center of the room).  From the cart in the rear portion of the room, you will need to take to your lab station the following items:

a.      2 Dissecting microscopes

b.     2 Plastic Petri dishes

c.     A plastic pipette for each member of your group

d.     One identification book for each group

e.      Optional:  plastic tupperware container, turkey baster

 

Stream Collection Procedure:

 

1.     The outside pair will need:  1 poking device, 1 non-white tub, and 1 net for collection.

2.     Find a stream location somewhere between the quad and highland drive.

3.     One student will use the poking device to stir up the creek sediment, turn over rocks, or other forms of dislodging the organisms into the water.

4.     The other student will position themselves approximately a half meter downstream from the person stirring up the creek.  Make sure that you are positioned directly downstream, moving the net up and down in the water column, as well as side to side as the stirrer moves the sediment.

5.     Do this for approximately 1 minute.

6.     When finished, lift the net out of the water and immediately place the net in the plastic tub to ensure that all of the organisms fall into the tub.  Carry the tub back to the classroom with the net completely within the boundaries of the tub.  (Keep in mind that you may not see any organisms in the net  theyre pretty small and will be difficult to see until you get back into the lab.)  Please go into the building through the main door leading to the science department, and wipe your feet carefully and thoroughly on the mat.

 

Identification of Macro-Invertebrates Procedure:

 

1.     Bring the net and tub to your lab station and place the tub in the sink, still with the net within the tub boundaries.  Carefully turn the net inside-out over the tub, and run tap water through the net until the tub is about half full.  Your goal is to get all of the little invertebrates out of net and into the tub.

2.     Remove the tub from the sink and set on top of the lab table at your station.  Leave the wet part of the net hanging over the sink for the remainder of class.

3.     You will have some leafy debris in your tub, so take the leaves out of the tub, but make sure you slide your fingers over the leaves to get any bugs off of the leaves.

4.     Let the remaining sediment settle, and look closely for movement.

5.     Using either the small pipettes or the turkey baster, suck up any organisms that you see and put them directly onto the Petri dish, or into the plastic Tupperware container.

6.     Using the identification book, try to determine the order of the organisms that you find (speckle winged quill mayfly, little yellow stonefly, riffle beetle, etc).

 

Clean-up:

 

1.     Put all of the little organisms back into the tub, and have a group member take the tub back to the location that you collected.  Dump the contents of the tub back into the stream.

2.     Clean up your lab station by putting the microscopes and other materials back in the cart.

3.     Leave the net over the sink.

4.     Wipe down the lab station with a sponge.  Make sure there is no dirt or debris on the floor, the lab tables, and the sinks.  Also make sure to wipe up any large water spills on the floor.
