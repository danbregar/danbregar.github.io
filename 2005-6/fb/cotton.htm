Cottonwood Questions

Field Biology

 

Prior to the 20th century, young cottonwood trees were far more abundant along the Willamette River than they are today (cottonwoods are trees that prefer to grow in wet areas).  Why have young cottonwood trees become much rarer?  Use the Upper Willamette Valley Landscape paper to answer this question.

 

Here is the procedure you should follow in researching your answer:

1.   Go through the text and find all the references to cottonwoods.

2.   Read and evaluate these references in the text – do they help answer the question?  If so, continue on to step 3.  If not, don’t waste any time trying to interpret them!

3.   Take the words in the paper and use them to come to a complete, clear understanding of the processes and concepts that are discussed:

a)   Translate appropriate sentences from “scientistese” to English so that the words are meaningful to you.

b)   Visualize the processes and concepts that are brought up (for example, what is “channel constraint”?  What about a “flooding regime”?  What happens to a river when it its channel is NOT constrained?  What does it look like when a flood occurs?).  Imagine yourself actually present as these things occur, and try to describe the types of things you would see as a result.

c)   What did you learn from this paper?  How do the processes and concepts discussed contribute to the growth of young cottonwood trees?  Why might these be important ideas to know – how can they help you understand more about ecology in general?
