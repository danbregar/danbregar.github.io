Nitrogen Inquiry Activity

Field Biology

 

In groups of three, do the following:

 

1.   Come up with a “What is the relationship between…” question that addresses soil nitrogen levels (either NO3- or NH4+) and some other measurable quantity (such as plant cover, soil moisture level, soil texture, distance from the creek.

 

2.   Describe a simple system you can use to take your measurements.  Address the following:

a)   Where will you collect soil samples?

b)   How will you measure the other quantity?

You do not need to discuss how you will measure the soil nitrogen – we will be working through this together in class.

 

3.   Using a trowel and a plastic bag, collect one soil sample from each of your sites (collect a maximum of 3 soil samples per group).  Make sure to take soil from inconspicuous areas and replace your divots!

 

4.   Return to the classroom – get a clean Petri dish and make sure that your names and the sample location are written on the bottom as shown on the board.  (Use a wet erase marker).

 

5.   Using a 3-beam balance, find the mass of your (empty) Petri dish – write it on the bottom of the dish as shown on the board.

 

6.   Fill your Petri dish with soil.  Then find the mass again and write it on the bottom of the dish as shown on the board.

 

7.   Place your Petri dish in the drying oven for 24 hours.

 

8.   Prepare a data sheet with your group member’s names, the question you were asking, and the following data fields for each of your site locations.

a)   Mass of Petri dish empty (record this from your Petri dish)

b)   Mass of Petri dish with wet soil (record this from your Petri dish)

c)   Mass of Petri dish with dry soil

d)   Mass of wet soil alone

e)   Mass of dry soil alone

f)    % moisture of original sample = (Mass of wet soil – Mass of dry soil)* 100/ Mass of Dry soil

g)   Ammonium level (mg/L)

h)   Nitrate level (mg/L)

 

9.   Remove your Petri dish and record the final mass on your data sheet.

 

10. Grind your soil sample thoroughly using a mortar and pestle.

 

11. Add 60 mL of water to 10 mL of ground soil (use two graduated cylinders).  Shake well.  Let sit for 10-15 minutes.  Use the nitrate and ammonium probes to measure nitrate and ammonium levels; record on your data sheet.
