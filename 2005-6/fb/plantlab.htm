Laboratory Assignment:  “Grow a Plant”

 

This laboratory will give you a sense of plant growth and how it differs with different types of soil and different amendments.  Keep in mind that one soil may require more care than another soil.  Although many people enjoy gardening and they do not consider extra time spent in gardening as a concern, we are going to assume that you are growing this plant for a living.  Your time should be considered a “labor input”.   Therefore, in this laboratory we will not only compare the growth of plants that grow in different soils, we will also compare the amount of care that these plants will require.  We will determine the amount of care that is required by recording the amount of water that is added to each soil over the three week period. 

 

You will work individually in this assignment.  Each person may select either the potting soil or the sandy soil.  They can then select one of several amendments:  Bat Guano (10-3-1);  Bone Meal (0-10-0); Miracle Gro (15-30-15); or sphagnum peat moss (no analysis).  Sphagnum moss is added to help retain moisture in a soil.  It is especially useful in a sandy soil.

 

If you want to add more than one amendment to your soil or if you have one from home that you would like to add to your “experiment”, check with Mr. Nesse.

 

Put your initials on your plastic tube with a permanent marker.  Add enough soil to your 3 and ½ inch plastic tube so that it is about 2/3 full. Mix your amendment (according to directions on the amendment) into the top two inches of soil. Place the soil and tube on a plastic plate.   Using a paper cup, add enough “aged” water so that water is dripping out the bottom of the tube. Add more water to make sure that all of the soil is wetted.  Take the excess water that has collected on the plastic plate and pour back into the paper cup.  (The aged water is water that has been left out overnight to allow chlorine to evaporate.)  Take one plant seed and gently place it about ½ to 1 inch below the surface of the soil.  A small seed should be ½ inch from the surface, while a large seed such as a corn or bean seed should be 1 inch from the surface.  The seed should not be touching the side of the plastic tube.   The seed should be covered with soil.  (Do not compact.  If it is compacted, water will not infiltrate properly into the soil.)

 

Make sure that you record the date and the amount of water that you add to your  tube on each watering date.  (Measure the water that is added each time.)  Subtract the amount that seeps through. Each of you will need to be responsible for your own growth tube and the survival of your plant.  Your teacher will be observing!  Add water when the surface soil is not moist.  Always check your growth tubes on Friday.

 

There will be several “pots” of soil that will not have any amendments added to them to give you an idea of the success of your “farming”!
