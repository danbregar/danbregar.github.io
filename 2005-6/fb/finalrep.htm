Final Report Guidelines

Field Biology

 

Here is the format for your final report.  Each person needs to submit a final report – this is an individual assignment.  Please include the headings for each section in your paper and make sure that the headings are in the order indicated below.  The due date for your final draft is Thursday, June 15.

 

Question:

This should include a revised version of the question/purpose you stated in your proposal that reflects any changes you have made as your project has progressed.  This section should be in past tense when it refers to things that you have now completed.

 

Methods:

This should be a detailed, step-by-step list of your methodology.  This should be completely updated from your proposal and include any additions or modifications you made to your proposed methods.  Include citations where appropriate.  This section should be in past tense when it refers to things that you have now completed.

 

Results:

This section should include a numeric summary of your data.  Numerical data should be entered into a spreadsheet as a table.  You can copy the data into a word processor for your final report; however, it is acceptable to simply print a copy of the spreadsheet as your data.  The printed version should be neat, easily read, and clearly labeled and formatted.  Where appropriate, calculate average values for your measurements.  Where useful, create a graph or chart for your data.  Since each group is collecting a different type of data, you should check with your instructor frequently to make sure that your data table is in an acceptable format.

 

Discussion:

This section is the most important part of your report. 

 

Your discussion should address the following points:

1.      Is there a correlation between the elements you studied?

2.      If so, is it a positive or negative correlation?

3.      Does your R-squared value indicate a strong or weak correlation?

4.      Do you think there MIGHT be a cause/effect relationship between the elements you studied?  If so, what is it, and WHY do you think that relationship exists? 

 

References:

Include a list of references in standard format.
