Study Guide – Soil Quiz

Field Biology

 

1.   Why are soil organisms important to the ecological health of plants growing in the soil? 

 

2.     Why might a soil sample have a large amount of NH4+ and a small amount of NO3-?  What kinds of changes could you make to increase the amount of NO3- and decrease the amount of NH4+?

 

3.     Which process of the nitrogen cycle rely on oxygen?  In highly aerobic (oxygen-full) soils, what nitrogen products would you expect to see a lot of?  In highly anaerobic (oxygen-poor) soils, what nitrogen products would you expect to see a lot of?

 

4.     Explain how soil organisms can contribute to the decomposition of organic material at the surface of the soil.

 

5.     In the winter, would you expect CV’s soil to have a high amount of NO3-?  Why or why not?

 

6.     What do you think might happen to nitrogen levels in the soil if you removed all of the organisms (like macroinvertebrates) that take large chunks of organic matter and chop them down into much smaller pieces without changing the form of the nitrogen in those pieces?  Why?

 

7.   A chunk of soil is removed from the back seat of Sammy Cole’s car and placed in a glass beaker.  The mass of the soil and the beaker together is 425 grams.  The soil is dried for two days in a drying oven; after it has dried, the mass of the soil and the beaker together is 388 grams.  The mass of the beaker alone is 78 grams.  What is the water content of this soil?
