Study Guide - Willamette Valley Ecology

Field Biology

 

1.     Before European-American settlers came to the Willamette Valley, how did Native Americans influence the native vegetation along the Willamette River bottomlands?  Why did the Native Americans do these things?

 

2.     The climate of the Willamette Valley might be described as "warm and dry".  Why is this an accurate description, despite the fact that here in Corvallis we have a large amount of rainfall during the winter and spring?

 

3.     Why has the amount and manner of flooding in the Willamette Valley changed so much over the past 100 years?  Discuss the impact of dams and human modifications to the river channel.

 

4.     How (and why!) has the changed flooding regime in the Willamette River impacted cottonwood stands?

 

5.     Why do coniferous forest communities now dominate so many areas that were previously occupied by prairie and oak savanna?

 

6.     The Crescent Valley area, prior to the 20th century, was an ash swale.  Describe the vegetation and hydrology of the area as it appeared in 1850 and compare that to the vegetation and hydrology of the area now (hint – some of this information was discussed in class; some of it should come from your own personal observations of the Crescent Valley area).

 

What is the meaning of the following terms:  lateral migration, channel constraint, riparian community, garry oak community, prairie community, coniferous forest community, ash swale
