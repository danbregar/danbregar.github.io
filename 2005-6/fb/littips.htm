Literature Review Tips

Field Biology

 

1.   Do not plagiarize

a.   If you don’t know what something means, don’t write it down

b.   If you know what something means but can’t figure out a better way to say it, put it in quotes

c.   If it’s not in quotes, it needs to be 100% your own words

2.   Follow the template/format

3.   Find a REAL scientific paper (that describes the methods and results of a study conducted by real scientists)

a.   Use Google

b.   Use scientific names of plants and animals

c.   Use the word “study” or “experiment”

d.   Use EBSCO

e.   Search for people

f.    Look for sites affiliated with city, state, or federal government; universities; private research foundations

4.   Summarize AND evaluate

5.   Don’t be overly general (“This study was about plants”)

6.   Don’t necessarily try to read every word in the paper

7.   If you’re struggling to find a paper, try to find some references in another paper, then search on the references or the author’s name
