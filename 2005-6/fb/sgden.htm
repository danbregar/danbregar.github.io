Study Guide – Denizens of the Soil

Field Biology

 

1.   In Europe, oak trees can grow in sandy soils or clay soils.  Describe where most nutrients are stored in oak forest systems that grow in each type of soil.

 

2.   Why does litter decompose so rapidly in oak forests that grow in clay soil as opposed to oak forests that grow in sandy soil?

 

3.   Use pictures to describe the setup, methods, and results of Joe Anderson’s soil experiment.  Make sure to label the amount and location of nutrients (nitrogen).

 

4.   Describe in your own words how soil invertebrates regulate decomposition and nutrient recycling in the soil.

 

5.   What does “BPGT” stand for?

 

6.   What do you have to measure in order to be able to “read” information (such as location, altitude, forest type) from a soil sample?

 

7.   Why is the cyanide-producing millipede so important in Northwest forests?  What do you suppose would happen if that millipede disappeared?

 

8.   In your own words, describe Moldenke’s four morals to this article.
