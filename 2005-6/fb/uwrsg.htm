Study Guide – Upper Willamette River Landscape Paper

Field Biology

 

1.   According to the abstract, approximately what percentage of the original Willamette River channel between Eugene and Albany has been lost since the 1840’s?

 

2.   In the section "Willamette River Channel Loss", the author claims that this section of the Willamette River has been changed from a multiple channel system to a simplified/single channel system.  What does she mean by "multiple channel system", and how did this change occur?

 

3.   According to the cited paper by Boyd (1986), why did Native American tribes in the Willamette Valley burn some of the timbered bottomlands along the river?  How did this change the plant and animal communities in the burned areas?

 

4.   Explain how channel constraint and reduced flooding might impact cottonwood stands along the Willamette River.  In your answer, explain the term “lateral migration”.

1.   How and why did burning maintain the Garry Oak communities in the Willamette Valley?  Why are unburned areas now dominated by coniferous forest communities (mostly Douglas Fir)?

 

2.   In general, what types of actions would need to be taken in order to restore wetlands in the Willamette Valley?
