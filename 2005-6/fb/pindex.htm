Phosphorus Index

 

Into the blanks for the P index, assume that your soil has the following situation:

 

Assume

1)  Medium erodibility of 3 tons per acre per year

2)  Moderate Permeability, 1 – 5 percent slope

3)  Poorly drained soil with a 1 – 3 foot seasonal high water table

4)  Silt loam soil

5)  Field is separated form surface water, and water runs off the field into surface water only during very intense rainstorms.

6)  Soil Test P2O5 = 10 ppm

7)  P is incorporated (mixed into the soil) < one week after application

8)  50 lbs. of P2O5 is applied.

9)  100 lbs. of  Manure P is being added

 

What is the P Index? _________

What is the risk level? __________

 

One at a time, make the following changes and see if you can get the index to sneak into the Very High Risk level.

 

Ø      Reset the Soil Permeability Class to very slow and then check the P Index and risk level by clicking on “see results”

Ø      Reset Soil Permeability to Moderate 1-5%.  Change “P is incorporated < one week after application” to P is “Surface applied to frozen or snow covered ground – Check risk level by clicking on “see results”..

Ø       Reset application method to “< in week after application”. Change the P Soil Test level to 75 ppm and check risk level.

 

Do any of these raise the risk level to very high?  Which one?_____________________
