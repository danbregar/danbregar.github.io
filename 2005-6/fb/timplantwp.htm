Information for Timberhill Plant Web Page

Field Biology

 

Here are the requirements for your web page and the associated files:

 

1.   You should have a folder on your H: drive to hold all of your pictures and documents.  The name of this folder should be “Your_Plant” – use the common name of your plant, with each word capitalized, and separate the words by spaces.

2.   In your folder, you should have a 8” wide, 100 dpi picture of your plant saved in JPEG format.  The name of this file should be “Your_Plant.jpg”.

3.   In your folder, you should have a 5” wide, 72 dpi picture of your plant saved in JPEG format.  The name of this file should be “Your_Plant-5.jpg”.

4.   In your folder, you should have a 1” wide, 72 dpi picture of your plant saved in JPEG format.  The name of this file should be “Your_Plant-icon.jpg”.

5.   In your folder, you should have your web page* (created in Microsoft FrontPage or Microsoft Word).  The name of this file should be “index.htm”.

6.   Once all your work is completed, copy your web page to the FB folder on the Student File Server (accessible from my web page).

 

*Check to make sure that your web page contains the following components:

1.   The local name (as used at Timberhill) of your plant.

2.   The 5” photo of your plant.

3.   Your name and the date your web page was created.

3.   The full scientific name (spelled correctly!) and additional common names for your plant.

4.   Important and/or interesting data from USDA PLANT database (cited correctly!)

5.   Links to at least three additional internet sources (use Google).  For each source, include a couple of sentences describing the type of information someone would find at your site.

6.   Scanned or transcribed references from Hitchcock, Gilkey, and/or Haskin (cite cite cite!)

7.   Your web page should be formatted so that all text is centered.  The font should be Times-Roman.  The local name of your plant should be font size 24 and bold; the rest of the information should be in font size 16.

Ferocious Riley

 

Canus rileyous:  Deadly Riley, Hungry Riley, Common Legbiter

 

This web page was made by Dan Bregar on December 4th, 2005.

 

Here are some web sites that are great sources for information about Ferocious Riley

“Riley – Loves to Eat!” – http://www.rileythedog.com/eating.htm

This site is a great overall description of Riley.  It explains her most common food sources, and talks about why she is so vicious.

 

“The Visual Riley” – http://www.picturesofriley.com

This site is a warehouse of all the pictures of Riley that have been published on line or in print.  It contains over 2000 pictures and new ones are being added each day!

 

“I survived Riley” – http://www.imasurvivor.com

This site is the true account of someone who survived an encounter with Riley in the wild.  This person (a student at Crescent Valley High School) was nipped over 7 times in the heels and ankles by Riley while she was trying to herd him.  Luckily, he was not injured, and lived to tell the tale.  This site also discussed some of the most common myths about Riley.
