Kincaid’s Lupine Project

Field Biology

 

Agenda for Thursday, 10/13/2005

 

Jen Cramer from the Institute for Applied Ecology led the students through the seed preparation procedure (including scarification and stratification).

 

The general procedure we followed was:

1.   Count the seeds in your envelope.

2.   If necessary, label a germination container, using masking tape, with every piece of information on the envelope.

3.   Scarification – pinch a seed between your fingers and gently and slowly rub it across the sandpaper.  Try to minimize seed breakage.

4a. If your seeds will be direct-planted, put them back in your envelope and label it “Scarefy”.

4b. If your seeds will go into a germination container, load them into the germination container at this time.

-     Cut a blue sheet so that it’s a little smaller than the tray, get it damp, and put it in the container.

-     Make 5 rows of 5 seeds evenly spaced (25 total seeds per germination container).

5.   Put your information onto the data sheet on the computer in the front of the classroom.
