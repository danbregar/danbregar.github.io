“Make Your Own Soil Column”

 

You will make one and hopefully two columns each for testing permeability.  The first column should be a “homogenous” soil – use one kind of soil media only.  If you have time for a second column,  use the same media for the second column but add one or more  soil layers over the top of it (but the total amount of soil would equal one cup) or add leaves to the top of the soil.  You are welcome to try to make a column that take “forever” to drain!

 

1.  Each of you need the following:

Ø      one clear mailing tube/column with a capped end containing holes

Ø      one steel wool pad

Ø      one 12 ounce paper cup of media (Corvallis silt loam soil, Newport Beach sand, Douglas fir large bark, Douglas fir bark mulch, pea gravel)

Ø      it is O.K. to have two or more soil layers of different media for your second column (for instance, a half cup of sand over a half cup of soil) but please have them add up to one total cup of media just so that we can better compare our results.

Ø      You may also take a bag of leaves to put on top of your media.

Ø      Two - 300 ml beakers or a 300 ml beaker and a 300 ml (at least 250 ml) graduated cylinder

Ø      A rod for pushing the steel wool down to the bottom of the column

 

2.  Place the steel wool pad in the mailing tube. The steel wool protects the holes from clogging with soil.  Push it to the bottom of the tube.

 

3.  Pour one cup of media over the steel wool.  

 

4.  Add 250 milliliters of water.  Check the time that the water was added.

 

5.  Note the time that the water quits coming out the bottom of the column.  Subtract and enter the time that it took to go through the soil to the table on the board.

 

6.  Prepare a second column if time permits.  (you have about 30 minutes for this exercise).  For the second column, dump the soil in the appropriate “spent soil” container.  Save the steel wool by washing it in the sink after shaking off excess soil.
