Electronic Poster Guidelines

Field Biology

 

Each group is to create a poster version of the group’s final research report.  Posters must be completed using Microsoft PowerPoint.  The purpose of the poster is to highlight each of the main sections of your research paper.  Email or otherwise submit an electronic copy of your poster to Mr. Bregar by Friday, June 9.  (This is a group assignment – you only need to submit one poster per group.)

 

You should include lots of pictures and maps to make your poster inviting.  The text on your poster should be short (bulleted rather than paragraph format), easy to read from 8-10 feet, and should allow anyone to get an overall impression of your project goals and accomplishments.  Your posters should be visually attractive and contain all of the following information:

 

Your poster should have the following components:

 

Title (5 points)

·        This should closely match the title of your final group paper.  Somewhere near the title, list your names (in a smaller font).  Also, if your project was done in cooperation with other individuals or agencies, you should list them somewhere near the title.

·        The font size for the title should be larger than any other font on the poster.

 

Question (10 points)

·        The question or purpose your group addressed (if necessary, include enough background information that your viewers can easily comprehend the question/purpose).

 

Methods (10 points)

·        A summary of your methods (if your group had several different methods, make sure to clearly indicate which part of your question is addressed by each method).

·        You may not have enough room (or wish to) include every step of your procedure.  Should someone want to know these, they can look at your paper or simply ask.

·        Your general procedures should be discussed.

 

Data (10 points)

·        You need not list these as “results”.

·        You should display your data as a table

 

Graph (10 points)

·        This is where good charts and graphs are critical when attempting to display correlations between your dependent and independent variables

·        Remember – charts and graphs done well should be completely self explanatory, clear, and simple.

 

Analysis (10 points)

·        Answer the following questions:

1.      Is there a correlation between the elements you studied?

2.      If so, is it a positive or negative correlation?

3.      Does your R-squared value indicate a strong or weak correlation?

4.      Do you think there MIGHT be a cause/effect relationship between the elements you studied?  If so, what is it, and WHY do you think that relationship exists? 

 

 

Specific Requirements:

1.      Use Microsoft PowerPoint.  Go to Page Set-up and select a paper size of 48” wide by 36” high.  This is known as “E-sized” paper.  It is best to make the electronic version of your poster as large as possible.  If you make it smaller and then later wish to enlarge it, the fonts you define in your poster will look “blocky”.

2.      Use white backgrounds behind black text – this is generally the easiest to read.

3.      Locate on the bottom, left hand corner of your poster a “Revised:  DATE” line and the name of the file.  Use a small font size – 10 or 12 point for this. 

4.      While you want to be complete in your description, do not include excessive amounts of text.  The text font should be sized so that information is easily read from eight to ten feet away.

1.      Be sure to spell check and proofread.  THIS IS CRITICAL.  Many people will be looking at your posters.  Points will be deducted for simple mistakes.

2.      Use the posters that are posted in the hall of the science department as examples. 

3.      Keep in mind that when most people read your poster, very few will start with the title and then work their way through the poster in the order listed below.  The eyes of your reader will generally be drawn to large text, graphs, pictures, and diagrams.  Ensure that these things stand alone as much as possible.
