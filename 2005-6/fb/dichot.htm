Dichotomous Key

Field Biology

 

Using ten of the plants that we have looked at in class, create a dichotomous plant key.  A dichotomous key uses a series of questions to successively divide a group of plants (or other organisms) into halves until a particular species is identified.

 

Working with a partner, you should create a dichotomous key for any ten of the plants that we’ve looked at in class.  Your key should satisfy the following requirements:

 

1.   Each plant should be identified by a unique series of questions

2.   Each plant should be identified by its correct common and scientific name.  Scientific names are always written in italics, with the first word capitalized and the second word lower case (for example, Elymus glaucus).

3.   Questions may be used more than once, in different sections of the key – but they will be used to separate different groups of plants!

4.   Each question should be identified by a number or a number and a letter, and the two answers to each question should direct the reader to either a specific plant species or to another question.

5.   Your questions should be geared towards the types of visual observations someone might make from a plant specimen in the classroom.
