Agenda for Monday, October 17, 2005

Field Biology

 

1.   Spend ½ hour working in small groups (2-3) on the Willamette Valley Historical Ecology Quiz study guide (posted on my web site).  Remember – the key to success on this quiz is to have a clear understanding of the concepts and processes – so your best bet is to visualize what is going on.  For example, when thinking about how the Native Americans burned the landscape, picture yourself standing up on a hill, actually watching one of these burns take place.  What gets burned?  What ends up still alive?  What does the land look like one day after the burn?  What does it look like six months after the burn?

 

2.   Spend ½ hour going over the answers to the Willamette Valley Historical Ecology Quiz study guide as a group.

 

3.   Spend the last ½ hour preparing an individual dichotomous key that you can use on the plant quiz on Friday.  You can work in groups of 2-3 to prepare this key, but each of you needs your own copy!  In order for you to be able to use this key on the quiz, your key needs to meet the following criteria:

a.   It should be a properly formatted dichotomous key, as we discussed in class

b.   It should have ONLY scientific names on it – no common names will be allowed

c.   It should have no other information on it except the visual descriptions of the plants necessary to identify them.  (In other words, your key should NOT refer to information such as whether the plant is native or non-native, or what type of soil the plant grows in.)

d.   It needs to be turned in to Mr. Bregar by Thursday, October 20, so he can review it prior to the quiz.
