Inquiry Project Brainstorming

Field Biology

 

For your aquatic invertebrate inquiry project, we will be answering a question or group of questions as a class.  Your questions need to be ones that can be addressed in a relatively short time period (we will only have about 1 hour for data collection), must be possible to answer at this time of year, and must involve aquatic macroinvertebrates as at least one of the elements being studied.

 

Your questions will be in the form “What is the relationship between (measuring word) of (element one) and (measuring word) of (aquatic macroinvertebrates)?”.  In other words, your question format will be similar to the format that you developed last week regarding your year-long class project.

 

Today, you will work in pairs to generate several different question ideas.  Here is the process you should follow:

 

1.   List ten possible things that could potentially interact with aquatic macroinvertebrates.  Think about both living and non-living components of the ecosystem – such as water temperature, specific plants, and objects in the water.

2.   Come up with some “measuring words” – words or phrases that indicate what you could measure about something and how (in a very general way) you might measure it.  Some examples of measuring words are “dried mass”, “temperature”, “stem length”, and “presence or absence”.  Try to avoid using words that describe something difficult to measure directly (such as “health” – how would you actually go about measuring the health of an insect or plant?).

a.   List ten different measuring words you could use for aquatic macroinvertebrates.

b.   List five different measuring words for TWO of the elements you listed in question 1 (for a total of ten measuring words).

3.   Create a list of five “What is the relationship between” questions – each question should use a different measuring word that applies to aquatic macroinvertebrates and should use a different second element (from question 1).
