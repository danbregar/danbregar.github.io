Final Presentation Guidelines

Field Biology

 

Here is the format for your final presentation.  (The dates and details of the final presentations will be given later.)  Each person should speak between 2-3 minutes; when your group reaches its limit, I will cut you off (as nicely as possible). Each group member should contribute equally during the presentations.  The audience will be composed of students, parents, school administrators, mentors, and interested community members.

 

Your grade will be compiled from evaluations by Mrs. Nunes and the rest of the class.

 

Requirements:

1.   Must be created in PowerPoint (one presentation per group)

2.   Include all of the relevant information from your study, including:

a.   Introduction (2 – 3 slides total for your question, your background information, your hypothesis, and your site location)

b.   Methods (1 - 2 slides, should be in past tense)

c.   Results (1 - 2 slides total for your raw data and calculated numbers – include hypothetical data if appropriate)

d.   Analysis (2 - 3 slides total for your statistical analysis, conclusions, generalizations, and implications of your study – should be in past tense where appropriate)

e.   Acknowledgements (1 slide) - thank everyone who provided you with help

3.   Should be a minimum of 2 minutes and a maximum of 3 minutes per person

4.   Each group member should be prepared for detailed questions from audience members

 

Important Notes:

  • I have a digital camera you can use to take pictures of your sites and your project.  This is a great way to improve the visual appeal of your presentation without distracting from the overall content.

 

  • You may use notecards and outlines to help with your presentation.  However, each group member should have memorized at the very least the following information:

 

    • What is the question your group was trying to answer?
    • From a scientific point of view, why was this study important?
    • What were the methods you used to answer your question?
    • What did your data show?
