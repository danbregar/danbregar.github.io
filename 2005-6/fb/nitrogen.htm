Nitrogen Inquiry Activity

Field Biology

 

In groups of three, do the following:

 

(on Friday, March 11)

1.   Come up with a “What is the relationship between…” question that addresses soil nitrogen levels and some other easily measurable quantity (such as plant cover, soil moisture level, soil texture, distance from the creek).  We will be collecting all of our soil from the area near Jackson creek in between the E and F buildings (by the photography room).

 

2.   Describe a simple system you can use to take your measurements.  Address the following:

a)   Where will you collect soil samples?  (Remember to limit your data collection to near the photography room)

b)   How will you measure the other quantity?

You do not need to discuss how you will measure the soil nitrogen – we will be working through this together in class.

 

3.   Using a trowel and a Tupperware container, collect one soil sample from each of your sites (collect a maximum of 3 soil samples per group).  Make sure to take soil from inconspicuous areas and replace your divots!

 

4.   Return to the classroom – get one clean Petri dish for each of your sites and make sure that your names and the sample location are written on the bottom.  (Use a wet erase marker).

 

5.   Using a 3-beam balance, find the mass of your (empty) Petri dishes – write it on the bottom of the dishes.

 

6.   Fill your Petri dish with soil.  Then find the mass again and write it on the bottom of the dishes, and place your Petri dishes in the drying oven for the weekend (make sure the temperature is set for 35 C).

 

7.   Prepare a data sheet with your group member’s names, the question you were asking, and the following data fields for each of your site locations.

a)   Mass of Petri dish empty (record this from your Petri dish)

b)   Mass of Petri dish with wet soil (record this from your Petri dish)

c)   Mass of Petri dish with dry soil

d)   Mass of wet soil alone

e)   Mass of dry soil alone

f)    % moisture of original sample = (Mass of wet soil – Mass of dry soil)* 100/ Mass of Dry soil

g)   Ammonium level (mg/L)

h)   Nitrate level (mg/L)

 

8.   Clean up – make sure your Tupperware containers are cleaned out and leave them in the back to dry.  Wipe off the counters, wipe down the sinks, and pick up any dirt or mud you might have deposited on the floor.

 

(On Monday, March 14)

9.   Remove your Petri dish and record the final mass on your data sheet.

 

10. Grind your soil sample thoroughly using a mortar and pestle.

 

11. Add 60 mL of water to 10 mL of ground soil (use two graduated cylinders).  Shake well.  Let sit for 10-15 minutes.  Use the nitrate and ammonium probes to measure nitrate and ammonium levels; record on your data sheet.
