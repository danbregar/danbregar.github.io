Agenda for 10/21/05

Field Biology – 4th Period

 

You will have 15 minutes to take the plant quiz today.  During the other 30 minutes of class, here is your task:

 

1.   With a partner, brainstorm at least three different elements (aspects of the environment such as a certain plant, an insect, soil, water, etc.) that you could study at Chip Ross Park (or Bald Hill, if our study site changes).  Write these on the board so the rest of the class can see them.

2.   Pick three of these elements (they can be yours or they can be others that are written on the board) and list at least five different measuring words that could be applied to each.  (For example, you could measure the height of a plant, the amount of rainfall, or the mass of a soil sample.)

3.   On a sheet of scratch paper, write two questions (one for you and one for your partner) that represent a scientific research project that you could do at our study site.  Make sure that, to the best of your knowledge, these questions are ones that can be answered given the time, material, financial, and logistical constraints of this class.  For example, you will not be able to do a study that would require yearly measurements of plant growth, because we will only have one year to do our study.  You will also not be able to do a study that looks at the mass of Kincaid’s Lupine plants because we will not be able to harvest these plants in order to weigh them.

 

When done, write your name and your partner’s name on the sheet of paper and turn it in to the turkey bin.  Gobble, gobble gobble.
