Agenda for 3/16/06

Field Biology

 

You have two main tasks today:  to locate and record GPS coordinates for possible data collection sites on campus, and to complete the methods section of your proposal.  Here are the details for each task.

 

Task 1: Locate and record GPS coordinates for your campus data collection sites.

            You will be collecting data for your year-long project both at Bald Hill and here on campus.  Today, you should spend 15 – 20 minutes tracking down at least 5 possible spots on CV’s campus you could use to collect data for your project.  Use the GPS receivers to find the GPS coordinates of these sites – press the “Page” button (on the upper right-hand corner) until you see the big compass, then press the “down arrow” button (on the left-hand side) until the window at the bottom of the receiver says “Location”.  Make sure to record these numbers carefully, as they will be a part of your proposal.  Keep in mind that this task does NOT need to be finished before you start Task 2 – so if other students are using the GPS receivers, you should be working on Task 2 while you wait!

 

Task 2: Complete the methods sections of your proposal. 

            The format is on my web site (posted March 10).  There are four main parts of the methods section – your step-by-step list of instructions (Checkpoint 3 – which you’ve already completed); your data sheet (which many of you have already completed); a list of sites where you will collect data (in other words, the GPS coordinates you collected in Task 1) and an aerial photo of Bald Hill and CV’s campus (which you should finish today).  To find the aerial photos, use the Benton County GIS site (http://gis.co.benton.or.us/v09_10/Source/Container.htm) – 1st, zoom in to the spot where R5W and T11S intersect on the map.  Then, to find CV, zoom in around the number 14.  To find Bald Hill, zoom in around the number 32.  To add aerial photography to the map, click on the plus sign next to “Legend/Layers”.  Then click on the plus sign next to “Aerial Photography”.  Then click the check boxes next to “Aerial Photography” and “Benton County – July 2005 – color 1 ft”.  Then, click on the “Redraw map” icon.  Once the aerial photos show up, you can zoom in closer if you need to.  You should save these images to your H: drive – just right-click on the images and choose “Save Picture As…”.  You should save the pictures in Bitmap (.bmp) format so we can easily edit them later.

 

I will be checking off your methods sections at the beginning of the period on Friday – so you should have all of this work completed by then.  If you do not finish everything during class today, make sure to finish up at home.
