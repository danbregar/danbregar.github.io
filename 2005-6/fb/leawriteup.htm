5. Methods

Write one (1) paragraph describing how we collected the data to answer the question. You can write in complete sentences or you can use bullets. For example:

  • First, we bought 16 cans of purple paint.
  • Second, we painted all the windows purple.
  • Third, we applied a second coat of paint.

 

6.  Results

PART I: Present the data we collected. You should at least include a data table and one graph. Make sure to include labels, units, etc.

 

PART II: Interpret the data we collected. Use complete sentences. Write one (1) paragraph that answers these questions:

1.      Now that we have collected our data, how would you answer our research question?

2.      What do you think the results indicate about your group’s hypothesis?

(Can you say the hypothesis was right? Wrong? How/why?)

      3. What were possible sources of error in our data collection?

 

7. Conclusion

Use complete sentences. Write one (1) paragraph that considers these things:

  1. What is the ecological significance of our results? How might another scientist find these results useful?
  2. If you were going to do this experiment again, what might you change?

(For example: What would make your life as a researcher easier? OR What would make your data more meaningful? OR What would make the experiment more interesting?)
