Answer the following questions on your own sheet of paper.  For questions 2-5, you should write roughly 1 – 2 paragraphs per question.

 

There will be a graded discussion on Friday concerning these topics.

 

1.   Define the following terms:  Gene Frequency, Evolution, Speciation through evolution, Natural Selection

 

2.   In what way is the debate over evolution theory a “semantic argument” (an argument caused by differences in definitions of words or concepts)?  What can be done to resolve this dilemma?

 

3.   Using the information we have discussed in class, evaluate the following statement:  “Evolution … refers to the theory that the first living organism developed from nonliving matter.  Then, as it reproduced, it is said to have changed into different kinds of living things, producing ultimately all forms of life that have ever existed on earth, including humans.”

 

4.   Using the information we have discussed in class, evaluate the following statement:  “…the darker variety of peppered moth, which is said to be a mutant, survived better because it was difficult for birds to see them … the darker variety rapidly became the dominant type.  But was the peppered moth evolving into some other type of insect?  No, it was still exactly the same peppered moth, merely having a different coloration.”

 

5.   Describe, in your own words, the difference between a scientific theory and a non-scientific theory.  Feel free to inject your own opinion into this discussion.
